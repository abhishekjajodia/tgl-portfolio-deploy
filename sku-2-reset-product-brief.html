1. Name + Elevator Pitch

Name: Reset (working name; final lock pending TM clearance per TGL-P1-DEC-007 + naming sprint round 2 with Deepika).

Elevator pitch (3 sentences, repeatable by anyone on the team):

Reset is TGL's PCOS and hormonal-dysregulation SKU, designed for the 22-40 year old Indian woman diagnosed with PCOS, PCOD, or "borderline PCOS" who is searching for a credible Ayurveda-rooted alternative to Metformin and OCPs. Three plant-cellulose capsules a day, split across morning, lunch, and dinner with meals, deliver 1000mg of clinical-dose Methi (vendor-network standard 4-5% saponins) for insulin sensitivity, 400mg Shatavari for cycle regularity, 300mg Curcumin C3 Complex (Sami-Sabinsa) for the inflammatory side of PCOS, plus Manjistha, Bhringraj, BioPerine, and TGL's farm-grown moringa heritage thread to support cycle regularity, weight management, and acne over a 3-6 cycle arc. It's the second-highest-LTV SKU in the portfolio and the natural Q1 partner to Cycle: where Cycle is acquisition for the wider PMS audience, Reset is conversion for the diagnosed-PCOS subset that has done the research.

3. The customer (who she is, what she's tried, what fails her)

Who she is (core 26-34, edge 22-40):

She's an Indian woman in her late 20s or early 30s, urban Tier 1-2 (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune; expanding via WhatsApp-cluster word-of-mouth into Tier 2 and Tier 3 metros), household income ₹6-30L/year, English-Hindi (or English-Tamil/Telugu) bilingual. She is a working professional, a teaching or healthcare worker, a small-business owner, or in some cases a graduate student. She has been diagnosed by a gynaecologist or endocrinologist with PCOS, PCOD, or "borderline PCOS" / "lean PCOS" / "hormonal imbalance" — often via a hormonal panel + ultrasound that her doctor read back to her in two sentences.

She is a searcher. She has spent 6-24 months researching PCOS independently. She reads Reddit r/PCOS, follows Instagram PCOS-coach accounts, watches YouTube doctors. She has read scientific abstracts. She knows about insulin resistance, AMH, the LH/FSH ratio, why "lose 5 kg" is hard advice when your insulin resistance fights you on it. She is the most informed customer in TGL's catalog, and she will scrutinise the ingredient list.

What's happening to her body (the medical context):

Her cycles are irregular — sometimes 35 days, sometimes 60, sometimes she skips a month. Weight gains around her abdomen and stays. Acne reappeared in her late 20s after years of clear skin. Hair fall is creeping up; some hirsutism on her chin and upper lip is becoming hard to ignore. Her energy is lower than she remembers; her sleep is good some nights and broken others. She may have been told her AMH is high, her LH:FSH ratio is inverted, her insulin is rising. Her doctor offered Metformin and a birth-control pill. She has likely declined or hesitated on both: Metformin's GI side effects scare her; the birth-control pill feels like a band-aid that masks the symptoms while her body changes underneath.

She is in the decision phase — the 1-3 month window after diagnosis where she is hesitant on prescription medication, trying lifestyle changes (low-carb, intermittent fasting, more walking), and looking at supplements. This is the acquisition window for Reset.

4. What the product does (the lived experience over 3-6 months)

Reset is a long-arc product, and we say so directly. PCOS is a 6-month minimum journey; cycle changes show up at month 2-3; the full picture lands at month 6+. The single most important customer-education message for Reset is "PCOS is a 6-month journey. Most women see their first cycle change in month 2-3. Stick with it." Drop-off at Month 1-2 is the #1 retention risk; honest expectation-setting at the front of the journey is what protects the LTV math.

Here is what she actually experiences across a 6-month arc on 3 caps/day, split with meals:

Month 1: the adjustment window.

Minimal perceived change. Her body is calibrating to the Methi + Shatavari + Curcumin stack. The split-meal dosing (one cap morning, one cap lunch, one cap dinner) becomes a routine she has to build; the Methi can be slightly bitter on first taste through the cap shell. This is the highest drop-off risk for any PCOS supplement category. Marketing job in Month 1 is not to sell efficacy; it's to sell patience. Deepika's content arc in launch month centers on "PCOS doesn't get fixed in 30 days. Your cycle takes 60-90 days to respond. Reset is built for the long arc."

Months 2-3: the first cycle signal.

A meaningful proportion of consistent users start to notice:

  • Cycle landing closer to a 28-32 day rhythm (vs the 35-60 day variability of pre-supplement baseline)
  • Energy steadier through the day (the Curcumin anti-inflammatory layer + Shatavari adaptogenic action)
  • Acne-flare frequency reducing (the Manjistha + Curcumin pair working on the inflammatory pathway)
  • Cravings reducing slightly, particularly the post-meal sugar-crash crash (the Methi insulin-sensitivity layer)

The user pattern at this stage: she finishes Pack 2, recognises the cycle is starting to hold a rhythm, and decides "let me see if Pack 3 confirms it." This is the trial-to-loyal conversion moment. Subscription retention math says: if she finishes Pack 2 and reorders Pack 3, she is typically a 12-24 month customer.

Months 3-6: the full effect lands.

Cycle regularization meaningful (50-70% of consistent users per the Inositol + Methi + Shatavari literature; lower-bounded for our supportive-stack dose architecture). Weight stabilization or modest reduction in some users (Methi + Curcumin metabolic action). Acne fades for a substantial subset (the inflammatory-pathway hypothesis playing out). Hirsutism is the slowest marker — improvement, if it shows, lands at month 6+. Energy and sleep improvements compound.

6-12+ months: maintenance and stabilization.

Sustained cycle regularity. Some users start to consider conception with the cycle predictability they've earned. Acne and weight markers stable. She has settled into Reset as a daily ritual; subscription is on autopilot. She introduces friends in her PCOS circle to it.

6. Theoretical justification (why each active works)

For each of the 7 actives: mechanism, classical basis, modern clinical evidence with verifiable URLs, dose rationale.

1. Methi (Trigonella foenum-graecum) extract (4-5% saponins, 333mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: Trigonelline + 4-hydroxyisoleucine + furostanol saponins + galactomannan fiber. Insulin-sensitizing via multiple pathways: improves GLUT-4 translocation in skeletal muscle, reduces postprandial glucose spike, modulates lipid profile (HDL up, LDL down), supports ovarian function indirectly via insulin-axis improvement.
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita classifies Methi as Vrishya + metabolic herb. Strong food-medicine continuum in Indian dietary practice (methi is in dal, in parathas, in pickle).
  • Modern clinical evidence: Sreelatha S et al. "Antioxidant activity of Trigonella foenum-graecum using various in vitro and ex vivo models." Phytotherapy Research, 2009; 23(2):139-145. PubMed. Plus Bawadi HA et al. "Inhibition of Caco-2 colon, MCF-7 and Hs578T breast, and DU 145 prostatic cancer cell proliferation by water-soluble black bean condensed tannins." Phytomedicine, 2009. Critically, Swaroop A et al. "Efficacy of a novel fenugreek seed extract (Trigonella foenum-graecum, Furocyst) in polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)." International Journal of Medical Sciences, 2015; 12(10):825-831. PubMed — Methi extract at ~1g/day showed PCOS-specific cycle regularity + ovarian-cyst regression at 90 days.
  • Dose rationale: 333mg/cap × 3 caps = 1000mg daily extract. At 4-5% saponins, delivers 40-50mg saponins/day — at the lower-clinical floor of the Swaroop 2015 PCOS RCT saponin dose range. Generic vendor-network 4-5% spec (NOT Furocyst's specialty 40% spec) per DEC-016; saponin dose math works at commodity grade.

2. Shatavari (Asparagus racemosus) extract (4-5% saponins, 133mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: phytoestrogenic steroidal saponins (shatavarins I-IV) bind weakly to estrogen receptors. In PCOS context, supports HPO axis modulation, particularly relevant for FSH/LH ratio normalization (PCOS shows elevated LH:FSH ratio). Adaptogenic action on the HPO axis.
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, Bhavaprakasha Nighantu. Primary Stree-rasayana; explicit reference for cycle-regulation in classical women's-wellness texts.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Pawar VA et al. — Phytotherapy Research, 2012, small clinical study showing Shatavari supplementation supports cycle regularity in oligomenorrhea (irregular periods, a core PCOS feature). Plus Alok S et al. "Plant profile, phytochemistry and pharmacology of Asparagus racemosus." APJTD, 2013; 3(3):242-251. PMC.
  • Dose rationale: 133mg/cap × 3 caps = 400mg daily extract. Delivers 16-20mg shatavarins/day, classical therapeutic range for cycle-regulation context in PCOS.

3. Curcumin C3 Complex (Sami-Sabinsa branded, 95-97% curcuminoids, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: curcumin (the principal curcuminoid in turmeric) is a polyphenol with strong NF-kB modulation, anti-inflammatory cascade interruption, and antioxidant action. PCOS has a documented chronic low-grade inflammatory component (elevated CRP, oxidative stress markers); curcumin addresses that inflammatory axis directly.
  • Classical basis: Turmeric is foundational in Ayurvedic anti-inflammatory protocols (Haridra); the curcumin extract is the modern-isolated active.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Aggarwal BB et al. — extensive published lab and clinical evidence base on curcumin anti-inflammatory action; the C3 Complex specifically has been the subject of multiple RCTs across inflammatory conditions. Sohrab G et al. "Effects of curcumin supplementation on metabolic profiles in patients with polycystic ovary syndrome." Phytotherapy Research, 2021. PubMed — curcumin at ~500mg/day showed PCOS-specific improvements in fasting glucose, HOMA-IR, total cholesterol over 12 weeks.
  • Dose rationale: 100mg/cap × 3 caps = 300mg C3 Complex daily = ~290mg curcuminoids/day at 95-97% standardisation. With BioPerine 15mg/day for 2000% bioavailability boost (Shoba 1998), effective absorbed dose is in the clinical-therapeutic range.

4. Manjistha (Rubia cordifolia) extract (~10% anthraquinones, 8:1, 25mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: anthraquinones + iridoid glycosides + phytoestrogen-like compounds. Pitta-pacification (Ayurvedic dosha framework) maps to anti-inflammatory + skin-clarity action. Direct relevance to PCOS-acne (cyclical hormonal acne is the most visible PCOS symptom for many customers).
  • Classical basis: Bhavaprakasha Nighantu — Manjishtha as Pitta-pacifying + Rakta-shodhaka (blood-purifying); strong classical basis for acne and cyclical skin disorders.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Mishra AP et al. "Therapeutic uses, pharmacology and phytochemistry of Rubia cordifolia." International Journal of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences, 2013. Limited modern RCT data specifically for PCOS-acne; mechanism-driven inclusion + classical evidence.
  • Dose rationale: 25mg/cap × 3 caps = 75mg 8:1 extract = ~600mg whole-root equivalent daily. Sub-clinical primary, supportive role for PCOS-acne and Pitta layer.

5. Bhringraj (Eclipta alba) extract (8:1 from whole-leaf, 25mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: wedelolactone + flavonoids + alkaloids. Classical adaptogen + hepato-protective; in PCOS context, supports the cortisol/stress overlay where chronic stress perpetuates insulin resistance. Also supports hair-fall (a common PCOS symptom) but Root SKU is the primary hair-fall product.
  • Classical basis: Bhavaprakasha — Bhringaraja as Rasayana + hepato-protective + hair-tonic.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Wagner H et al. — established phytochemistry literature on Eclipta alba alkaloid + flavonoid activity. Limited PCOS-specific RCT data; mechanism-driven + classical inclusion.
  • Dose rationale: 25mg/cap × 3 caps = 75mg 8:1 extract = ~600mg whole-leaf equivalent daily. Sub-clinical primary, supportive role for cortisol layer.

6. BioPerine (Sami-Sabinsa branded piperine, 95%, 5mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: piperine inhibits hepatic glucuronidation and intestinal P-glycoprotein efflux, dramatically increasing systemic absorption of co-administered actives — particularly curcumin (2000% bioavailability lift per Shoba 1998).
  • Modern clinical evidence: Shoba G et al. "Influence of piperine on the pharmacokinetics of curcumin in animals and human volunteers." Planta Medica, 1998; 64(4):353-356. PubMed. Foundational paper for BioPerine + curcumin co-administration.
  • Dose rationale: 5mg/cap × 3 caps = 15mg piperine/day. Cross-SKU standard bioavailability dose; primary purpose in Reset is curcumin absorption boost.

7. Moringa (Moringa oleifera) whole-leaf powder (heat-treated, TGL captive supply, 25mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: whole-plant nutritive (iron, calcium, vitamin C, B-complex, antioxidants, chlorophyll). Not a primary PCOS-symptom active; supports overall nutritional baseline + brand heritage thread.
  • Folk + modern basis: established nutraceutical literature on moringa as iron + folate + B-complex source. PCOS customers are often vegetarian and often borderline-anaemic; the moringa background nutritive contribution is a relevant supportive layer.
  • Modern evidence: Alegbeleye OO. "How Functional Is Moringa oleifera? A Review of Its Nutritive, Medicinal, and Socioeconomic Potential." Food Nutr Bull, 2018. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 25mg/cap × 3 caps = 75mg daily total. Tier A heritage signature dose ceiling per Moringa Foundation principle (calibrated against Deepika's stacking-overload concern when summed across Reset + Cycle + Crest + Bloom in a heavy stack).
  • Brand role: heritage signature carrying the cross-portfolio narrative.

7. How we arrived at this product (the decision narrative)

Three layers of decisions that produced Reset as the SKU it is.

Layer 1: Why Reset as Phase 1 Q1 (not Phase 2)

We evaluated all 10 SKUs across volume, profitability, TGL-fit, and founder authenticity. Reset scored highest on profitability (longest LTV after Crest) and category-volume (PCOS is the largest single concern category in women's wellness) and high on Phase 1 launchability:

  • Warm cohort overlap with Cycle. TGL's existing 28-38 customer base overlaps the 22-40 Reset cohort by ~70%. Cycle acquires the wider PMS audience; Reset converts the diagnosed-PCOS subset that Cycle's content surfaces.
  • Bundle architecture unlock. Phase 1 as Cycle + Bloom + Reset (DEC-012 expansion from 2-SKU to 3-SKU plan) unlocks three bundles vs one in the original plan. Reset is the third leg that makes the Q1 launch a portfolio.
  • Latent demand validated. The Customer Reviews Repository signals the strongest existing demand within TGL's customer base — repeated mentions of "doctor confirmed no PCOS after 6 months" using TGL's existing single-ingredient moringa. Reset gives the existing base the formulation depth they're already manufacturing themselves.

So Reset was sequenced to Phase 1 Q1, making it the second-highest-profile launch alongside Cycle and Bloom.

Layer 3: Why this specific dose architecture (and not another)

Every dose was triangulated across three constraints:

  1. Clinical / classical therapeutic range (what RCTs or Bhavaprakasha actually use)
  2. Capsule fill feasibility (Size #0 = ~700-1000mg practical fill at our 496mg fill, very comfortable)
  3. 3-cap-per-day split-meal protocol (DEC-014 lock — preserves therapeutic Methi dose without forcing oversized Size #00 cap)

That triangulation forced the 3-cap path: Methi 1000mg/day clinical PCOS dose cannot fit in a 2-cap protocol at Size #0; the alternative would have been a Size #00 × 2-cap protocol with granulation, which compromises the split-meal blood-level consistency the Methi saponins benefit from. The 3-cap protocol is the explicit trade-off — accepted as a UX cost for therapeutic dose preservation and split-meal pharmacokinetic fit.

Trade-offs we accepted, openly stated:

  • 3-cap protocol vs 2-cap UX target. Reset is one of two Tier A SKUs (alongside Nourish in Tier B) that fails the 2-cap UX target per DEC-014. We accept the higher pill burden because the PCOS-savvy customer is more dose-fluent than the average wellness customer; she will read "1000mg Methi clinical dose" and respect the protocol.
  • Generic 4-5% Methi saponins, not Furocyst 40% specialty. DEC-016 vendor-network discipline. Furocyst would give us a stronger marketing claim (40% saponin spec sounds more credible than 4-5%) but at the cost of single-supplier concentration risk + higher COGS. Saponin dose math works at commodity grade.
  • No D-Inositol in V1. The strongest single-active PCOS evidence base sits outside our Phase 1 launch because the natural-source Indian supply chain isn't mature. We counter the missing-Inositol perception with the strong Methi + Curcumin + Shatavari triangle; V2 ships Inositol when supply matures.
  • Manjistha and Bhringraj at sub-clinical doses. 25mg each is below the 50-100mg standalone clinical doses for Pitta-pacification and adaptogenic action. We use them as supportive layers; the Curcumin handles primary inflammatory action and the Methi handles primary insulin axis.
  • Per-active mg numbers look small on label (Manjistha 25mg/cap reads weak). Mitigated by labelling clinical context ("Manjistha 75mg/day — supportive cyclical-acne support") and 8:1 standardisation marker (75mg of 8:1 = 600mg whole-root equivalent).

The decision trail in one sentence: Reset is the comprehensive PCOS SKU Indian women have been asking for at the ₹1,299 sale single-bottle premium-but-accessible tier; the 7-active stack pairs the strongest PCOS-specific Ayurvedic + Methi insulin-sensitivity intervention (Methi + Shatavari at clinical doses) with the under-addressed inflammatory axis (Curcumin C3 Complex + BioPerine at clinical anti-inflammatory dose), supportive Pitta + cortisol layers (Manjistha + Bhringraj), and the moringa heritage thread, on a 3-cap split-meal protocol that the PCOS-savvy customer will recognise as a real protocol rather than a simplified pill.

All 7 actives FSSAI-permitted under Schedule VI of the Health Supplements Regulations 2022. Methi permitted as common spice + nutraceutical (Annexure A). Curcumin permitted as common spice extract (Annexure A; Sami-Sabinsa C3 Complex is FSSAI-registered branded ingredient). BioPerine permitted as common spice extract (Annexure A; Sami-Sabinsa BioPerine is FSSAI-registered branded ingredient). Manjistha and Bhringraj permitted (Annexure B). Shatavari permitted (Annexure B). Moringa permitted.

Permitted claim language: "supports women's hormonal wellness," "supports healthy menstrual cycle," "helps maintain hormonal balance," "supports healthy glucose metabolism," "traditionally used in Ayurveda for women's reproductive wellness," "with clinical-dose Methi and branded Curcumin C3 Complex," "for women managing PCOS" (consumer use-case language), "Built on Moringa. Powered by Ayurveda."

NOT permitted: "treats PCOS / PCOD," "cures hormonal imbalance," "reverses insulin resistance," "replaces Metformin / OCPs," "restores ovulation."

Subtle but critical: TGL must NOT use "PCOS" or "PCOD" in product claims, taglines, or label copy as medical-condition-cure language. Permitted: "for women managing PCOS" (consumer use-case language), "supports women's hormonal wellness." Pack art can reference "for hormonal balance" but not "PCOS treatment."

Mandatory label compliance:

  • "NOT FOR MEDICINAL USE"
  • "CONSULT YOUR PHYSICIAN BEFORE USE IF PREGNANT, BREASTFEEDING, ON MEDICATION (ESPECIALLY FOR DIABETES, THYROID, OR HORMONAL CONDITIONS)"
  • "NOT INTENDED TO DIAGNOSE, TREAT, CURE, OR PREVENT ANY DISEASE"
  • FSSAI license prominent
  • Veg symbol, manufacturer/marketer details

Pregnancy / breastfeeding contraindication: Reset stack contains Manjistha (avoid in early pregnancy per classical caution) and Curcumin (high-dose curcumin is uterotonic at therapeutic levels) — explicit pregnancy contraindication on label.

Drug interactions to flag:

  • Diabetes medications (Metformin, sulfonylureas, insulin): Methi has hypoglycemic effects; combining with hypoglycemic medication can cause additive blood-glucose lowering. Mandatory physician-consult flag.
  • Hormonal medications (OCPs, HRT, fertility drugs): Shatavari phytoestrogen action; physician consult mandatory.
  • Anticoagulants (warfarin, aspirin): Curcumin mild antiplatelet effect at high dose; physician consult flag.
  • CYP450 substrate medications: BioPerine (piperine) inhibits hepatic CYP450 enzymes; can affect plasma levels of co-administered medications (particularly relevant for chronic-medication users). Physician consult mandatory.
