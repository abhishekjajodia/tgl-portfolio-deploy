1. Name + Elevator Pitch

Name: Crest (working name; final lock pending TM clearance per TGL-P1-DEC-007 + naming sprint round 2 with Deepika).

Elevator pitch (3 sentences, repeatable by anyone on the team):

Crest is TGL's menopause and perimenopause SKU, designed for the 45-58 year old Indian woman navigating hot flashes, sleep disruption, mood swings, and bone-density concern, who has declined HRT and is searching for a credible natural-source alternative. Two plant-cellulose capsules a day, every day, deliver SoyLife soy isoflavones (IFF Health Sciences), Shatavari, Aquamin algae calcium, Yashtimadhu, Vitex, and TGL's farm-grown moringa heritage thread to ease vasomotor symptoms, support sleep and mood, and protect bone density over a 6-month-plus arc. It's the long-LTV anchor of TGL's women's life-stage portfolio and the premium price-anchor of the Phase 1+ catalog: once a customer commits to Crest, she stays for years.

3. The premium price-anchor of the portfolio. Crest at ₹1,799 MRP / ₹1,599 sale / ₹1,359 sub is the highest-priced SKU in the Phase 1+ catalog, and that's the point. 3. The customer (who she is, what she's tried, what fails her)

Who she is (core 45-55, edge 42-60):

She's an established Indian woman, urban Tier 1-2 (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune; expanding via WhatsApp-cluster word-of-mouth into Tier 2), household income ₹15-50L/year, English-Hindi (or English-Tamil/Telugu) bilingual, with a stronger Hindi/regional comfort than younger TGL segments. She is a senior professional, a transitioning advisor or business owner, or a homemaker whose adult children have launched. She has 20+ years of supplement-buying history. She knows which brands she trusts and is suspicious of new entrants — but will switch when a product clearly improves her experience.

She is sandwiched: aging parents need care, adult children still reach for help, her own body is changing in ways nobody warned her about. She is wellness-aware but not Instagram-driven. She trusts her sister, her college friend, her sister-in-law, and her WhatsApp groups. Founder-personal content + peer testimonial wins her over; paid creator content does not.

What's happening to her body (the medical context):

Perimenopause begins in her early-to-mid 40s. Cycles become irregular. Hot flashes appear (30-80% of menopausal women experience them, with frequency and intensity varying widely). Sleep fragments — she wakes at 3 AM and cannot return. Mood becomes less predictable. Cognitive complaints emerge ("brain fog," word-finding pauses). Vaginal dryness begins, often unspoken. Bone density loss accelerates at the menopausal transition; her DEXA scan may already show osteopenia. Cardiovascular risk rises post-menopause as estrogen's protective effect withdraws. Body composition shifts — weight gain especially in the abdomen — with metabolic slowdown.

She is not pathologically diagnosed. She is in a normal physiological transition that her culture has historically suppressed ("just deal with it"). Her doctor has likely offered HRT (hormone replacement therapy); she has likely declined for some combination of cultural resistance, breast-cancer concerns inherited from older HRT generations, and a "natural is better" instinct.

4. What the product does (the lived experience over 6 months)

Crest is a long-arc product, and we say so directly. Phytoestrogen-stack effects build over weeks to months; bone-health benefits compound over years. The single most important customer-education message for Crest is "plan for 6 months minimum to see the full effect." Drop-off at Month 1-2 is the #1 retention risk; honest expectation-setting at the front of the journey is what protects the LTV math.

Here's what she actually experiences across a 6-month arc on 2 caps/day:

Month 1: the adjustment window.

Minimal perceived change. Her body is calibrating to the phytoestrogen and adaptogen stack. Maybe one or two nights of marginally better sleep. Maybe a slightly less intense hot flash. Often, nothing she would consciously register. This is the highest drop-off risk in any menopause supplement category. The marketing job in Month 1 is not to sell efficacy; it's to sell patience. Deepika's content arc in launch month centers on "menopause is a multi-year journey. Crest is built for the long arc, not the quick fix. Give it 6 months."

Months 2-3: the first signal.

A meaningful proportion of consistent users start to notice:

  • Hot flash frequency reducing 15-25% from baseline
  • Sleep onset easier, mid-night wake-ups less frequent
  • Mood less reactive
  • Energy steadier through the day

The user pattern at this stage: she finishes Pack 2, recognises she's been sleeping a little better and feeling slightly steadier, and decides "let me see if it sticks." This is the trial-to-loyal conversion moment. Subscription retention math says: if she finishes Pack 2 and reorders Pack 3, she is typically a 24-month customer.

Months 3-6: the full effect lands.

Hot flash frequency reduction in the 20-35% range from baseline. Sleep quality measurably improved (her wearable confirms it). Mood stabilization. The "brain fog" lifts incrementally. Cyclical symptoms in late perimenopause smooth out. Bone-density support is doing its work in the background; she will not feel it but her next DEXA may register.

6-12+ months: maintenance and bone-health compounding.

Sustained symptom relief. Bone-density preservation continues to compound (this is years-long math). Cardiovascular markers stable. She has settled into Crest as a daily ritual; subscription is on autopilot. She introduces friends to it.

6. Theoretical justification (why each active works)

For each of the 6 actives: mechanism, classical basis, modern clinical evidence with verifiable URLs, dose rationale.

1. SoyLife soy isoflavones (IFF Health Sciences, 40% total isoflavones, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: Soy isoflavones (genistein 50%, daidzein 35%, glycitein 15%) are SERMs (selective estrogen receptor modulators). Mild estrogenic activity at receptor sites where estrogen is deficient (vasomotor symptom relevance) and mild antagonist activity where estrogen is excess. Strongest single-compound meta-analysis evidence base for hot flash reduction.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Howes LG et al. "Isoflavone therapy for menopausal flushes: a systematic review and meta-analysis." Maturitas, 2006. PubMed. Confirmed 20-25% hot flash frequency reduction over 12-24 weeks. Plus Taku K et al. "Extracted or synthesized soybean isoflavones reduce menopausal hot flash frequency and severity: systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials." Menopause, 2012; 19(7):776-790. PubMed. Dose-response 30-80 mg/day genistein-equivalent. Crisafulli et al. "Effects of genistein on hot flushes in early postmenopausal women: a randomized, double-blind EPT- and placebo-controlled study." Menopause, 2004. PubMed confirms 54mg genistein-equivalent dose-response.
  • Dose rationale: 100 mg/cap × 2 caps = 200 mg daily extract = 80 mg total isoflavones. Lower-therapeutic floor of the Taku 2012 clinical band.

2. Shatavari extract (4-5% saponins, 200mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: phytoestrogenic steroidal saponins (shatavarins I-IV) bind weakly to estrogen receptors. Modulates estrogen activity (agonist where deficient, antagonist where excess). Adaptogenic action on the HPO axis. Mild diuretic helps menopause bloating.
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, Bhavaprakasha Nighantu. Primary Stree-rasayana; explicit reference to Vriddhavastha (women's later years) in Charaka.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Pandey AK et al. "Impact of stress on female reproductive health disorders." Biomed Pharmacother, 2018; 103:46-49. PubMed. Plus Alok S et al. "Plant profile, phytochemistry and pharmacology of Asparagus racemosus." APJTD, 2013; 3(3):242-251. PMC.
  • Dose rationale: 200 mg/cap × 2 caps = 400 mg daily extract. Delivers 16-20 mg shatavarins/day, classical therapeutic range for menopause-context phytoestrogen modulation.

3. Aquamin-Ca (algae Lithothamnion, 150mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: multi-mineral algae complex (32% elemental Ca, 2.2% Mg, 70+ trace minerals). Documented bioavailability comparable to Ca citrate. Bone-density preservation in the menopausal-loss window.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Felice VD et al. "Aquamin: A Multi-Mineral Complex Sourced from Marine Algae." Magnes Res, 2018. Plus Aslam MN et al. "A Mineral-Rich Extract from the Red Marine Algae Lithothamnion calcareum Preserves Bone Structure and Function in Female Mice on a Western-Style Diet." Calcif Tissue Int, 2010. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 150 mg/cap × 2 caps = 300 mg Aquamin daily = ~100 mg elemental Ca/day. Supportive 10% RDA dose; designed to stack with dietary Ca + Bloom cross-sell rather than replace full RDA supplementation.

4. Yashtimadhu / Licorice extract (4% glycyrrhizin, 50mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: glycyrrhizin + flavonoid liquiritin + isoflavones. Mild phytoestrogenic action. Adrenal-supporting (cortisol modulation during menopause-stress overlay). Mucosal soothing for vaginal dryness over chronic use. Anti-inflammatory.
  • Classical basis: strong traditional Ayurvedic basis (Madhuyashti). Bhavaprakasha classifies Yashtimadhu as Rasayana with explicit women's-wellness application.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Hajirahimkhan A et al. "Botanical modulation of menopausal symptoms: mechanisms of action?" Planta Med, 2013; 79(7):538-553. PubMed. Confirms phytoestrogen + adrenal-modulating mechanism.
  • Dose rationale: 50 mg/cap × 2 caps = 100 mg daily extract = ~4 mg glycyrrhizin/day at 4% standardisation. Well below the 100 mg/day pseudoaldosteronism safety ceiling for chronic use. Label must show mg glycyrrhizin per serving + hypertension/heart-kidney/diuretic-co-medication contraindication.

5. Vitex / Chasteberry extract (0.5% agnusides, 25mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: dopaminergic action on the pituitary, modulating prolactin and indirectly supporting the LH/FSH axis. Strongest evidence in PMS (Schellenberg 2001) and irregular cycles; supportive role for late-perimenopausal users still experiencing residual cyclic disturbance.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Schellenberg R. "Treatment for the premenstrual syndrome with agnus castus fruit extract: prospective, randomised, placebo controlled study." BMJ, 2001; 322(7279):134-137. BMJ full text.
  • Dose rationale: 25 mg/cap × 2 caps = 50 mg daily extract. Sub-clinical primary, supportive role in the stack. Industry-standard 0.5% agnusides spec.
  • Safety: contraindicated in pregnancy, breastfeeding, dopamine-receptor medication. Label must reflect.

6. Moringa whole-leaf powder (heat-treated, TGL captive supply, 75mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: whole-plant nutritive (iron, calcium, vitamin C, antioxidants, chlorophyll). Not a primary menopause-symptom active; supports overall nutritional baseline during a phase when dietary absorption can decline.
  • Folk + modern basis: established nutraceutical literature on moringa as iron + folate + B-complex source. Bone-density, mood, and energy all benefit from background nutritional sufficiency.
  • Modern evidence: Alegbeleye OO. "How Functional Is Moringa oleifera? A Review of Its Nutritive, Medicinal, and Socioeconomic Potential." Food Nutr Bull, 2018. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 75 mg/cap × 2 caps = 150 mg daily. Tier A 50-75mg signature dose ceiling per Moringa Foundation principle. Substantive enough to carry the moringa thread.
  • Brand role: heritage signature carrying the cross-portfolio narrative.

7. How we arrived at this product (the decision narrative)

Three layers of decisions that produced Crest as the SKU it is.

Layer 1: Why Crest as Phase 4 (not Phase 1, not later)

We evaluated all 10 SKUs across volume, profitability, TGL-fit, and founder authenticity. Crest scored second-highest on profitability (longest LTV) and category-volume (40+ cohort is large and underserved) but scored lower on Phase 1 launchability:

  • Cold cohort at launch. TGL's existing 10K+ customers skew 28-42; menopause-stage is years away. Launching Crest in Q1 would mean cold-acquisition into an unfamiliar segment.
  • Creator partnerships needed. 5-7 women-health influencers in the 40+ demographic are the activation lever. Building that creator network takes 6-9 months.
  • The Q1 trio (Cycle + Bloom + Reset) funds the runway. Q1 launches prove the website-led acquisition motion, fund creator outreach, and seed the 40+ secondary cohort before Crest ships in Q4.

So Crest is sequenced to Phase 4 not because it's a weaker SKU but because it's a higher-friction launch that pays off long-term. The premium pricing also requires the Q1 trio to set the perception ladder — Cycle ₹899 + Bloom ₹999 + Reset ₹1,499 prepares the customer to read Crest ₹1,599 as "the next-tier option" rather than "the only option, why so expensive."

Layer 3: Why this specific dose architecture (and not another)

Every dose was triangulated across three constraints:

  1. Clinical / classical therapeutic range (what RCTs or Bhavaprakasha actually use)
  2. Capsule fill feasibility (Size #00 = ~700-1000 mg practical fill)
  3. 2-cap-per-day compliance ceiling (anything beyond 2 caps creates retention friction; menopause customers are particularly fatigue-sensitive)

That triangulation forced the Size #00 path (Size #0 cannot hold the SoyLife + Aquamin + Shatavari mass). At 600 mg fill, capsule remains comfortable and we have headroom for vendor-formulator to add Sesamin (50mg) or D3 (small mass) at trial-fill if needed.

Trade-offs we accepted, openly stated:

  • Lower-therapeutic floor on isoflavones (80mg/day vs upper 120mg/day). Higher dose forces cap fill near Size #00 limit. We accept the lower-therapeutic clinical band as the right balance of efficacy + cost + cap fit. The 89% GM at MRP gives us latitude to lift the dose later if reformulation evidence warrants.
  • Aquamin at supportive 100mg/day Ca, not full 400-600mg/day RDA replacement. Cap fill cannot accommodate a full Ca RDA without crowding phytoestrogen and Shatavari. We frame Crest as bone-health support that stacks with dietary Ca and Bloom cross-sell, not standalone osteoporosis prevention.
  • Vitex at sub-clinical 50mg/day. Industry clinical Vitex doses run 100-200mg/day. We use it at sub-clinical for residual perimenopausal cycle support, not as a primary active.
  • Single Indian distributor (Inhalation Health) for Aquamin concentration risk. Mitigated by DPR moringa-Ca fallback path retained as backup if Inhalation Health supply turns adverse.
  • Per-active mg numbers look small on label (Vitex 25mg/cap reads weak). Mitigated by labelling clinical context ("Vitex 25mg — supportive perimenopausal hormonal balance") and standardisation markers.

The decision trail in one sentence: Crest is the comprehensive menopause SKU Indian women have been asking for at the ₹1,500-1,800 single-bottle premium tier; the 6-active stack pairs the strongest Western single-compound evidence (SoyLife isoflavones from IFF Health Sciences) with Ayurvedic phytoestrogen and adaptogen heritage (Shatavari, Yashtimadhu) and natural-source bone-health (Aquamin), all carrying the moringa heritage thread, with portfolio-leading 89% GM at MRP earning the premium positioning.
