Spark is a customer-base portability play + the most genuinely unisex SKU in the portfolio — not a women's-hormonal-continuum core SKU. Three reasons it's Tier C:

  1. Customer-base portability: ~10-15K existing TGL Ashwagandha buyers in repeat cohort. Spark is the direct upgrade path. Tier C SKUs prioritise existing-customer LTV expansion over new-customer acquisition.
  2. Unisex use case: Spark is the cleanest unisex SKU in the catalog (55% female / 45% male). The Tier A continuum is women's-hormonal-led.   3. Cross-sell engine for Tier A: Cycle (PMS) + Bloom (PCOS) + Reset (Menopause) customers all suffer chronic stress — Spark is the "and your stress" attach. Bundle architecture per portfolio strategy doc.

2.3 Why now (Batch 3 of the 12-month rollout)

Per Phase 1 sequencing locked in TGL-P1-DEC-017, Batch 3 launches the Tier C duo (Spark + Glide) after Tier A women's hormonal core (Cycle / Bloom / Reset in Batch 1) and Tier B cross-sell anchors (Calm in Batch 2) have matured. Launching Spark Batch 1 would have left it standing alone as "another Ashwagandha capsule"; Batch 3 lets it stand on TGL's full multi-active brand equity.

2.4 Why this is the right Tier C SKU to build (vs another Tier C extension)

Three TGL existing single-ingredient heroes are candidates for Tier C extension: Triphala (Pulse), Turmeric (Glide), Ashwagandha (Spark). Spark is the highest-leverage of the three because:

  • Largest existing buyer cohort: TGL Ashwagandha buyers ≈10-15K vs Triphala buyers ≈5-7K vs Turmeric buyers ≈3-5K. Largest migration pool.
  • Largest cross-sell surface: stress is a co-symptom of every Tier A women's-hormonal SKU; PMS, PCOS, perimenopause all carry stress + fatigue burden. Joint pain (Glide) and gut issues (Pulse) have narrower cross-sell.
  • Largest unisex revenue ceiling: stress is the most cleanly unisex concern; Glide and Pulse skew female (joint pain peri-menopausal, gut issues women-prevalent). Spark unlocks the men's secondary segment most cleanly.
  • Strongest evidence base: Ashwagandha has 24+ published RCTs, Shilajit has growing clinical literature, Bacopa has cognition RCTs going back 20 years. Triphala and Turmeric have weaker / more contested evidence in their target indications.

3.6 Anti-personas (NOT for)

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women — Ashwagandha contraindicated in pregnancy (mild abortifacient potential per traditional Ayurvedic texts; modern safety data limited)

  • Patients on sedatives, benzodiazepines, alcohol-dependent — additive sedation risk with Ashwagandha

  • Severe iron-deficiency anemia — Shilajit has natural iron content; not generally a problem but lab-monitor recommended
  • Children under 18 — adult-only product
  • Acute clinical depression / psychiatric medication — Spark is not a treatment for mental illness; refer to psychiatrist

4. What It Does (3-6 Month Lived Experience)

Spark is positioned around adaptogenic recalibration — the customer should NOT expect overnight stimulation (that would suggest a synthetic mechanism). Spark's effect builds over 4-12 weeks as the HPA axis recalibrates and cellular energy substrates rebuild.

4.1 Week 1-2: subtle calm + sleep onset

  • Sleep onset latency reduces by 10-15 minutes — customer notices they fall asleep faster
  • Mid-afternoon energy dip is less severe — the 3 PM crash is dampened, not eliminated
  • Subjective sense of calm — less reactive to small stressors (email volume, traffic, kids)
  • NO immediate "energy boost" — this is intentional and is the Pillar 1 differentiator vs caffeine-loaded "energy" supplements

4.2 Week 3-6: cortisol pattern shifts

  • Sleep maintenance improves — fewer 3-4 AM wakeups, more continuous sleep
  • Morning energy improves — wakes feeling more rested even at the same sleep duration
  • Work focus improves — Brahmi (Bacopa) cognition effect kicks in; second-half-of-day mental clarity returns
  • Exercise tolerance improves — Shilajit cellular-energy effect; workouts feel less depleting, recovery faster
  • Stress reactivity dampens — same stressors, smaller emotional response

4.3 Week 7-12: full adaptogenic effect

  • Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) reduction — if customer self-tracked, would expect 30-40% PSS reduction (literature benchmark from Chandrasekhar 2012: 44% reduction with KSM-66)
  • Cortisol AM/PM rhythm normalises — measurable on saliva cortisol panel (rare in customer practice)
  • Hair shedding reduces (women) — telogen effluvium recovery, 2-3 month delayed effect of stress-resolution
  • Libido recovery (both genders) — secondary effect of cortisol normalisation
  • Sustainable energy — no caffeine-style spike-crash; flat energy curve through the day

4.4 Week 13-26: maintenance + compounding

  • Stable cortisol resilience — customer reports "I just don't feel as overwhelmed by everything anymore"
  • Sleep quality stable at improved baseline — 7-8 hrs continuous sleep most nights
  • Subjective wellbeing improvement — Multidimensional Fatigue Inventory (MFI) improvement
  • Stack synergy compounds with TGL bundles — customers on Cycle (PMS) + Spark report compounded hormonal-stress relief; Calm (sleep) + Spark covers full 24-hour stress + sleep cycle

4.5 What Spark does NOT do (managing customer expectations)

  • Does NOT cure anxiety, depression, chronic fatigue syndrome — these are diagnosed conditions requiring clinical care
  • Does NOT replace SSRIs / benzodiazepines / thyroid medication — Spark is supportive, not substitutive
  • Does NOT produce immediate "energy boost" — that would be a stimulant mechanism, which Spark is not
  • Does NOT increase testosterone in men beyond physiologic range — Shilajit dose at 250mg/day is below the Pandit 2016 testosterone-effect dose (500mg/day); Spark does not make T-claim on label
  • Does NOT work for everyone — adaptogen response is variable; ~70% of users in clinical trials see meaningful PSS reduction, ~30% are non-responders. Customer should reassess at Week 8.

5.3 The triple formulation switch — full explanation (CRITICAL)

V3's reformulation per TGL-P1-DEC-025 made three ingredient switches vs the V1 dossier (Section 4 of dossiers/10-spark-ashwagandha-stress.md). Each switch swapped a brand-name premium ingredient for a generic-equivalent at materially lower cost, while preserving (a) the standardisation spec, (b) the formulation thesis, (c) the clinical evidence directional applicability. The strategic principle: Spark sells the FORMULATION + Ayurvedic stack story, NOT the brand-name actives.

12.5 Quality control gates per batch

  1. Raw material HPLC — incoming Ashwagandha extract (withanolides %), incoming Bacopin (bacosides %), incoming Shilajit (fulvic acid %) — reject if below spec
  2. Raw material heavy metals — Shilajit per-batch NABL testing (Pb, Hg, As, Cd) — reject if above ceiling
  3. Raw material microbial — total plate count, E. coli, Salmonella, aflatoxin (especially relevant for Shilajit + Bacopa wetland-source) — reject if above ceiling
  4. Raw material solvent residue — GC-MS testing for hexane, methanol residues — Pillar 1 zero-tolerance
  5. In-process blend uniformity — sampling 10 caps from blend, HPLC for active dose uniformity ±5% — re-blend if outside spec
  6. In-process capsule weight — automated weight check on capsule-filling line — reject capsules outside ±5% target weight
  7. Finished product HPLC — withanolides + fulvic acid + bacosides per finished pack — confirm within label spec
  8. Finished product microbial + heavy metals — final compliance check vs Schedule VI spec
  9. Stability testing — accelerated stability (40°C/75% RH × 6 months) on first 3 batches to confirm 24-month shelf life
  10. CoA archival — per-batch CoA archived in QC system + published on Shopify PDP per batch (Pillar 4 transparency)
