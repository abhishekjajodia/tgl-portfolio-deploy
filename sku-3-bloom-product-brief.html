Bloom is TGL's foundational daily women's multivitamin, designed for the 25-45 year old Indian woman who eats well-enough but knows her diet alone doesn't deliver the iron, B12, calcium, and Vit C her body actually needs. Two plant-cellulose Size #00 capsules a day, every day, deliver natural-source iron co-delivered through TGL's farm-grown moringa + Parry's organic spirulina, Plant Lipids' Amla 25% Vit C, Inhalation Health's Aquamin algae calcium, fermentation-derived methylcobalamin (true bioavailable B12), Avlaan's lichen-derived Vitashine D3, plus an Ayurvedic adaptogen stack of Shatavari + generic-grade Ashwagandha — no synthetic vitamin isolates, no Pillar 1 compromises. 3. The customer (who she is, what she's tried, what fails her)

Who she is (core 28-38, edge 25-45):

She's an Indian woman in her late 20s to mid-40s, urban Tier 1-2 (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune; expanding into Tier 2 metros via WhatsApp word-of-mouth), household income ₹6-25L/year, English-Hindi or English-Tamil/Telugu bilingual. She is a working professional, a small-business owner, a teacher, a homemaker who runs a household + side hustle, or a graduate student. She is wellness-conscious without being a wellness extremist.

She is a routinizer. She has Habits. She drinks a glass of warm water with lemon in the morning. She has tried green-tea-after-meals. She owns a copper bottle. She walks 6,000-8,000 steps a day on most days. She bought a Fitbit / Apple Watch in 2023-24. She follows Rujuta Diwekar OR Luke Coutinho on Instagram. She knows what "iron-deficiency anemia" means; she has been told by a gynaecologist or GP at least once that her hemoglobin is borderline.

What's happening to her body:

She eats well-enough — vegetarian or eggetarian with seasonal fish/chicken on weekends. Dal-rice or roti-sabzi most nights. Some seasonal fruits. She knows she should eat more leafy greens. She's tired more than she'd like, especially mid-afternoon. Her nails are brittle. Her hair shedding is "a bit more than it used to be." Her energy levels are inconsistent. She gets cold hands and feet more often. She bruises a little easily. She's been told by her doctor "your iron is borderline, take a supplement" — but she has resisted because the synthetic iron tablets the doctor prescribed gave her constipation and a metallic mouthfeel.

She is in the maintenance phase — not a clinical-condition phase. She doesn't have a diagnosis to anchor a high-CAC clinical SKU like Reset. She isn't in the cyclic-flare phase that drives Cycle purchases. She's in the slower-burning "I should be taking something daily for my baseline" phase. This is the acquisition window for Bloom.

What she's tried:

She has tried HealthKart Women's Multi (the Amazon-default ₹500 synthetic blend) and stopped after one bottle because "it didn't feel like anything was happening + the orange artificial smell was off-putting." She has tried OZiva Plant-Based Multi (₹899) and continued for 2-3 bottles because "it felt cleaner" but stopped because "I didn't feel a real difference." She has bought spirulina powder once, didn't know how to use it, and it sat on her kitchen shelf for a year. She has been recommended Patanjali Chyawanprash by her mother.

What fails her in the existing market:

  • Synthetic vitamin blends (HealthKart, mainstream pharmacy multis): the orange-artificial-flavour profile, the GI side effects of synthetic iron, the deep distrust of "this is just chemistry, not nutrition"
  • Half-natural multis (OZiva): close, but the Ayurvedic stack is light + the natural-source claim isn't fully delivered (synthetic D3, partly-synthetic B12)
  • Single-ingredient supplements (her own past spirulina powder, isolated iron tablets): no integration, no daily ritual, hard to remember
  • Doctor-prescribed iron: works clinically but the constipation + metallic mouthfeel breaks compliance within 2 weeks
  • The "everything bowl" approach (Chyawanprash, badam-pista, foods-not-pills): high cognitive load, low precision, slow

What she would buy: a daily women's multi that is (a) entirely natural-source — no synthetic vitamins, no synthetic iron salts, no chemical excipients — (b) delivers a meaningful slice of her daily iron, B12, Vit C, calcium, D3 needs (not necessarily 100% RDA, but honest natural-source delivery; her diet covers some of it), (c) includes Ayurvedic adaptogens that signal "this is rooted in something deeper than just chemistry," (d) is priced in the ₹900-1,200/bottle range that's premium-but-justifiable for daily use, (e) is two capsules a day (she will tolerate two; three is a stretch), (f) comes with a founder voice that explains why each ingredient is in there.

Bloom is built for exactly this customer.

4. What the product does (the lived experience over 3-6 months)

Format: Two Size #00 capsules a day, every morning with breakfast (or first meal of the day for absorption window — Vit C boosts iron absorption, fat-soluble D3 absorbs better with food). 60-cap bottle = 30-day supply.

Week 1-2 (acclimatisation): She takes 2 caps a day with breakfast. No GI side effects (no synthetic iron sulfate to upset her stomach). Faint earthy aftertaste from spirulina + amla — within tolerance. She doesn't feel a dramatic shift; she may feel slightly more energy mid-morning by day 5-7 if she's iron-deficient at baseline, but the signal is subtle.

Week 3-6 (subtle signal layer): Her brittle-nail issue starts to soften (slow nail growth means new growth shows clearer at the base by week 6). Hair shedding may reduce — the extracts work on the follicle's growth phase, which has a 2-3 month visibility lag. Mid-afternoon energy improves modestly. She notices she's not feeling cold-handed as often. None of this is dramatic; it's the subtle compounding signal.

Month 2-3 (the routine takes hold): This is the retention window. By this point, the bottle is part of her morning ritual. She has either re-ordered (if she was sub-confident at first) or moved to subscription (if she trusted the brand from day 1 — Cycle + Bloom bundle customers move to subscription faster). Hemoglobin recheck (if she does one at month 3) shows modest improvement — typically 0.3-0.7 g/dL in iron-supportive natural-source supplementation, vs 1-2 g/dL in clinical iron sulfate dosing. We do NOT promise hemoglobin lift; we promise "supportive natural-source iron alongside your diet."

Month 4-6 (the maintenance flywheel): She is now a daily-multi customer. She tells her sister, mother, college friend about it. She becomes a referral source. Hair density visibly improving by month 4-5 (the growth-phase signal lands at this lag). Energy stable. She no longer "thinks about" her multi — it's habit. This is the LTV unlock.

The 3-6 month claim is supportive replenishment, not therapeutic correction. We are clear with the customer that Bloom is NOT a clinical-iron supplement (if you have anemia, see a doctor), NOT a one-shot menopause fix (that's Crest), NOT a PCOS protocol (that's Reset). It's the daily multi that fills the natural-source nutritional gaps for the woman whose diet is already 70-80% there.

Founder voice (Deepika): "This is the bottle on my own kitchen counter. I take two every morning with my breakfast. I don't promise miracles — Bloom is the foundation, the maintenance layer. If you eat well most days and just want your multi to actually be plants instead of pharma — Bloom is built for you."

6. Theoretical justification (why each active works)

For each of the 9 actives: mechanism, classical/scientific basis, modern clinical evidence with verifiable URLs, dose rationale.

1. Moringa whole-leaf (heat-treated, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: whole-plant nutritive (natural Fe + Ca + Vit C + B-complex + chlorophyll + 90+ identified phytochemicals); galactagogue, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory.
  • Modern evidence: Alegbeleye, O.O. "How Functional Is Moringa oleifera? A Review of Its Nutritive, Medicinal, and Socioeconomic Potential." Food Nutr Bull, 2018. PubMed. 2023 systematic review on anaemia + pregnancy outcomes: PMC full text.
  • Dose rationale: 100mg/cap × 2 caps = 200mg/day. Tier A daily-multi anchor per Moringa Foundation tier calibration. Delivers ~1-2mg natural Fe + 30-50mg Ca + supportive B-complex + Vit C.

2. Spirulina platensis (organic, 250mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: complete protein + B-complex matrix + phycocyanin antioxidant + chlorophyll + non-heme iron. Note: spirulina B12 is largely pseudo-cobamides (not bioavailable) — does not deliver B12 RDA on its own.
  • Modern evidence: Karkos et al. "Spirulina in Clinical Practice: Evidence-Based Human Applications." Evid Based Complement Alternat Med, 2011. PubMed. Selmi et al. "The effects of Spirulina on anemia and immune function in senior citizens." Cell Mol Immunol, 2011. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 250mg/cap × 2 caps = 500mg/day. Modest dose for B-complex + protein matrix + non-heme Fe co-source (~7mg/day at 1.5% Fe content). NOT the primary B12 vehicle (that's methylcobalamin).

3. Amla Vit C extract (25% ascorbic equivalents, 200mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: natural-source ascorbic acid + tannins + polyphenols. Vit C primary: antioxidant, collagen synthesis co-factor, non-heme iron absorption enhancer (converts Fe³⁺ to Fe²⁺).
  • Modern evidence: Khan et al. "Vitamin-C content of Indian gooseberry (Phyllanthus emblica)." J Pharm Bioallied Sci, 2009 review. Plus Lynch & Cook "Interaction of vitamin C and iron." Ann N Y Acad Sci, 1980 — the foundational Vit-C-as-iron-absorption-co-factor paper. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 200mg/cap × 2 = 400mg extract = ~100mg ascorbic equivalents/day = 133% Vit C RDA. Co-located in formulation with iron sources to maximise absorption.

4. Aquamin-Ca (algae lithothamnion, 150mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: red algae-derived multi-mineral complex (~30% elemental Ca + Mg + 72 trace minerals). Higher bioavailability than synthetic Ca carbonate (Marigot proprietary mineralisation).
  • Modern evidence: Aslam et al. "Multimineral algae are more effective than calcium carbonate at improving bone health." J Med Food, 2010. PubMed. O'Gorman et al. "Aquamin and bone health." Nutrients, 2012. PMC.
  • Dose rationale: 150mg/cap × 2 = 300mg extract = ~100mg elemental Ca/day = 10% Ca RDA. Modest; Bloom is the supportive multi, not a calcium-primary supplement.

5. Shatavari extract (4-5% saponins, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: phytoestrogenic steroidal saponins (shatavarins I-IV); modulates estrogen activity (agonist where deficient, antagonist where excess); HPO-axis adaptogen.
  • Modern evidence: Pandey et al. "Impact of stress on female reproductive health disorders: Possible beneficial effects of shatavari (Asparagus racemosus)." Biomed Pharmacother, 2018. PubMed. Alok et al. "Plant profile, phytochemistry and pharmacology of Asparagus racemosus." Asian Pacific J Tropical Disease, 2013. PMC.
  • Dose rationale: 100mg/cap × 2 = 200mg/day. Below the 400-500mg therapeutic dose used in Cycle/Reset/Crest/Nourish (where Shatavari is the primary active); modest daily-multi-supportive dose.

6. Ashwagandha extract — generic 5% withanolides (100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: withanolides modulate HPA axis; reduces cortisol; supports thyroid function (mild); adaptogen.
  • Modern evidence: Salve et al. "Adaptogenic and Anxiolytic Effects of Ashwagandha Root Extract in Healthy Adults." Cureus, 2019 — primary KSM-66 cortisol RCT. PubMed. Generic 5% withanolides at therapeutic dose has same Schedule VI compliance + same withanolide pharmacology; KSM-66 evidence applies directionally at equivalent dose. Chandrasekhar et al. 2012 stress study at 600mg/day. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 100mg/cap × 2 = 200mg/day. Modest dose — Bloom uses Ashwagandha as a daily-multi adaptogenic touch, not a cortisol-primary clinical dose (that's Calm at 500mg/day KSM-66 OR Spark at 500mg/day generic). Per V3 cost lock, generic 5% (Vidya/Indfrag at ₹3K/kg) is the right grade for daily-multi role; KSM-66 (Ixoreal at ₹18.5K/kg) reserved for Calm + Spark where cortisol-specific claim is the SKU thesis.

7. Generic methylcobalamin API (1.5 mcg/cap)

  • Mechanism: bioavailable B12 (vs synthetic cyanocobalamin which requires liver conversion); supports neural function, red blood cell formation, methylation cycles.
  • Source: Indian methylcobalamin API suppliers (Mumbai/Surat) at IP-grade fermentation-derived methylcobalamin. NOT MecobalActiv-Sabinsa branded (that branding could not be confirmed in Sami-Sabinsa public catalog per V3 brand-attribution research). Indian API listings ₹130,000-200,000/kg — but use rate is sub-mg so per-cap impact is negligible (~₹0.0003/cap).
  • Modern evidence: Greibe et al. "Cobalamin in spirulina vs. fermentation-derived methylcobalamin." J Nutr, multiple studies on the bioavailability gap. Spirulina alone insufficient for B12 RDA per Watanabe et al. 2014.
  • Dose rationale: 1.5 mcg/cap × 2 = 3 mcg/day = 125% Vit B12 RDA. Modest above-RDA dose — allows dietary headroom for the woman whose diet is partly egg/fish-inclusive and partly vegetarian.

8. Vitashine D3 (lichen-derived, 400 IU equivalent/cap)

  • Mechanism: cholecalciferol (D3) — supports bone, immune, mood (D3 receptors are widespread). Lichen-derived natural-source vehicle (vs synthetic-equivalent or sheep-lanolin extracted).
  • Source: Vitashine UK origin (GHT Companies); India distribution by Avlaan Pharma (Chennai). 100,000 IU/g powder. IndiaMART listing confirmed at ₹36,000/kg. Use rate is sub-mg so per-cap impact is ~₹0.06.
  • Modern evidence: Heaney & Holick "Why the IOM recommendations for vitamin D are deficient." J Bone Miner Res, 2011. PubMed. Indian women have particularly high D3 deficiency rates per population studies — Harinarayan et al. 2007 + multiple Indian replications.
  • Dose rationale: 400 IU/cap × 2 = 800 IU/day = 100% D3 RDA. Adequate for daily-multi support; not the high-dose clinical correction (that's a doctor-prescribed regimen).

9. Curry leaf extract (carbazole alkaloids, 50mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: carbazole alkaloids (mahanimbine, koenimbine), antioxidant, traditional digestive + glucose-supportive. NOT iron-standardised — see V3 brand correction in Section 5 ⚠️ subsection.
  • Modern evidence: Nooron et al. "Murraya koenigii (curry leaf) — a review." J Med Plants Res, 2010 + multiple Indian phytochemistry papers.
  • Dose rationale: 50mg/cap × 2 = 100mg/day. Supportive brand-story phytochemical; the Indian-kitchen-heritage signal in the formulation. NOT the primary iron vehicle (that's moringa + spirulina co-delivery).

7. How we arrived at this product (the decision narrative)

Three layers of decisions that produced Bloom as the SKU it is.

Layer 1: The category decision (Q1 2026 strategic). Bloom exists because Cycle and Reset alone don't make a portfolio. Cycle is cyclic-use only (15-21 days/month). Reset is condition-anchored only (PCOS-diagnosed cohort). Neither delivers daily-routine retention. The Q1 launch needs a daily-continuous-use anchor that converts a Cycle one-off purchaser into a TGL subscriber. A daily women's multi was the obvious answer.

Layer 2: The natural-source-only decision (Pillar 1). Most multis on the Indian D2C market reach for synthetic vitamin isolates because it's cheaper and simpler to hit RDA. We rejected this because Pillar 1 is a brand-defining HARD constraint, not a marketing claim. Bloom is the proof point: even when the formulation discipline is hardest (multi category), TGL holds the line. This decision drives the choice of Vitashine D3 (lichen, not lanolin), methylcobalamin (fermentation, not synthetic cyano), Aquamin Ca (algae, not carbonate), and the moringa+spirulina iron-source approach (not ferrous sulfate). Cost: per-pack COGS ~₹176 vs ~₹40-60 if we used synthetic isolates. Premium positioning at ₹999 sale absorbs this gap.
