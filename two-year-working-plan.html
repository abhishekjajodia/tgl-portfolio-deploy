The Good Leaf: Two-Year Working Plan

The execution pathway for the next 24 months

Why this document exists

You asked for a structured two-year plan ahead of the LOI redraft. This is that document. It is deliberately not a financial model or a turnover projection. The honest answer is that we will know more at Month 3 than we do today, and more at Month 6 than at Month 3. Numbers in writing today would either be conservative enough to be useless or aggressive enough to be misleading. The value sits in the discipline of the execution pathway itself: who does what, by when, in what sequence.

Both of our payouts are tied to the brand's growth. That is what makes timelines and follow-through the spine of this plan. Numbers will populate themselves as the work compounds. We will revise this document at Month 3, 6, 12 and 18 with what we have actually learned.

The two engines of growth

There are two engines that decide whether a brand at our stage compounds or stalls.

The strategic engine

Insights, capabilities and capital. Customer research, competitive intelligence, marketing capability deployment, brand and product expansion, technology, working-capital float. This is what Boco brings. We run as much of this as we can through structured systems and tooling, so the engine compounds without scaling cost. That is how we can deploy at this depth on a brand at this stage of revenue without breaking the unit economics.

The execution engine

The human mindshare on the ground that makes the brand actually move. Marketplace conversations, content velocity, founder presence in vendor and partnership calls, day-to-day production oversight, regulatory follow-through. This is what Deepika brings as the fulcrum.

Neither engine works alone. Strategic insight without ground-level execution sits on slides. Ground-level execution without strategic insight is busy-work. The two engines are designed to compound each other.

Deepika as the fulcrum

The role is not a daily KRA list. It is the conscious effort across seven areas that gives the brand the tangible mindshare it needs to compound. Right intent and right motivation matter more than a checklist.

1. Marketplace relationships

Amazon Brand Team, Blinkit category managers, Flipkart KAM, Quick Commerce reps. Onboarding executions, listings refresh, weekly check-ins, escalations when stocks slip or visibility drops.

2. Founder content velocity

Founder-led Reels, day-to-day stories, the unscripted pieces only the founder can carry. Deepika's voice is the brand's most defensible asset. Cadence matters more than polish.

3. Media conceptualisation

Working with the Boco team on what creative gets made, what the story for each campaign is, what is authentic and what is not. Conceptual lead, not production lead.

4. Customer and operational communications

The voice on customer queries, vendor calls, partnership conversations. The exchanges where founder presence changes the outcome.

5. Production oversight

DPR-side production sequencing, batch planning, testing schedules, packaging coordination. The cost-plus margin structure rewards efficiency here directly.

6. Channel and partnership presence

Founder presence in calls with marketplace KAMs, distributors, channel partners, agencies. Conversations that need a founder, not a representative.

7. Regulatory follow-through

AYUSH certification, FSSAI renewals, NABL lab relationships, batch testing audits. The unglamorous work that keeps the brand operating cleanly.

The throughline across all seven: this is not transactional execution. It is the conscious effort that turns a brand into a compounding asset.

Boco as the strategic engine

What Boco brings sits in three layers.

Insights and analytics

Customer research, competitive landscape mapping, formulation strategy, product portfolio sequencing, marketing performance analytics, channel-level economics. The work that decides what to build and how to build it.

Capability deployment

Marketing (creative, performance, content production, brand asset development), brand (positioning, packaging, naming), technology (D2C site, subscription, customer data, marketplace integrations), supply chain coordination (vendor outreach, sourcing assistance), team and program management.

Capital deployment

Working-capital float for packaging procurement, raw material forward-supply where Boco floats ingredients, marketing spend ahead of revenue, technology, team and operating cost. Formalised as a 12 percent IRR preferred-return capital account so it is not an open-ended unstructured commitment.

Underneath all three: structured systems and tooling that compound. Most of the day-to-day operational infrastructure that makes this possible is non-headcount. That is how we plan to fast-track.

Profitability and margin orientation

Numbers are not in this document because they are premature. What is locked is the orientation:

  • Every SKU launches at a margin-led price point. We do not chase volume at margin loss.
  • Portfolio-average gross margin holds at 75 to 85 percent at retail across the 10 SKUs. The SKU floor is 75 percent.
  • The DPR cost-plus margin in the 10 to 15 percent band carries the real cost of factory upkeep and manufacturing volatility, without putting that load anywhere else.
  • The negative-ROI guardrail kicks in if any SKU or the multi-ingredient line as a whole is at sustained negative contribution margin three months in. We trigger a joint scaling review before pushing more capital at a problem that is not converting.
  • Marketing spend tracks against a defined ROAS floor per channel.

Profitability is a steady function of execution. It is not a separate workstream.

Cadence of working together

Boco shares: P&L summary, capital deployment ledger, marketing spend allocation, customer cohort and revenue trends. Deepika shares: production progress, batch yields, content programme, marketplace conversation status, regulatory progress. Anything off-plan gets surfaced and addressed in this meeting, not deferred.

Quarterly milestone review

Walk the phase exit signals against the actual state. Recalibrate the next quarter if the plan needs to flex. Update this document.

Semi-annual reset

At Month 6, 12 and 18: walk the full plan together, lock the next six months in detail, and revise this document to reflect what we have learned.

Ad-hoc operational

Weekly stand-up between the Boco TGL pod and Deepika for live escalations. Slack as the working channel.

What this plan is not

It does not lock revenue numbers or build a turnover model. That comes at Month 3 quarterly review, with first run-rate data in hand.

It does not define every operational detail. It defines the shape of the work, who carries which engine, and the phase exit signals. The detail gets defined inside each phase as we get there.

It is not a final word. The partnership agreement, the scope-of-work annex, and the cost-plus rate card are the binding documents. This is the working plan that runs alongside them.
