3. The customer (who he/she is, what he/she has tried, what fails him/her)

Who they are (core 35-65, peaking 40-55):

The Glide customer skews ~60% female / ~40% male — the most unisex SKU in the TGL portfolio. The 40-55 woman is the largest cohort: peri-menopausal joint laxity beginning, post-pregnancy joint stress accumulated over years, finger-joint stiffness emerging in her late 40s, knee discomfort on the morning stair-descent. The 35-50 man is the secondary cohort: weekend-warrior runner / yoga / gym, post-30 joint recovery slower than it used to be, lower-back stiffness from a desk-bound work week. The 55-65 segment of both genders is the early-osteoarthritis cohort: their orthopaedist has named the diagnosis but not prescribed DMARDs yet.

Household income ₹15-50L/year, urban Tier 1-2 (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune; expanding via WhatsApp-cluster word-of-mouth into Tier 2-3 — joint health is geographically broader than gut/hormonal). Comfortable with ₹600-1,500 monthly supplement spend. English-Hindi (or English-Tamil/Telugu) bilingual. He/she trusts ingredient transparency, brand-name actives they can Google (Curcumin C3 Complex / BioPerine specifically), and percentage standardisation labels.

What's happening to their bodies (the medical context):

Joint inflammation at this age band is a slow-onset multifactorial process: cumulative micro-trauma from decades of weight-bearing activity, age-related cartilage thinning, post-menopausal estrogen withdrawal accelerating bone-and-joint remodelling (in women), accumulated dietary inflammatory load, reduced sleep quality compromising overnight tissue repair, and the cortisol-stress-inflammation feedback loop. The morning-stiffness signal, the 1-hour-sitting-stiffness signal, the post-yoga-soreness signal — these are not pathological-yet, they are the body's pre-osteoarthritis communication.

He/she is generally not pathologically diagnosed (the anti-persona is the diagnosed RA / severe OA patient on prescription DMARDs — refer to clinician). He/she is in a normal aging-and-load physiological process that he/she wants to manage rather than treat.

4. What the product does (the lived experience over 3-6 months)

Glide is a slower-onset product than acute-pain customers expect, and we say so directly. Curcumin and Boswellia anti-inflammatory effects build over 4-8 weeks; joint-comfort improvement compounds over months as systemic inflammation reduces. The single most important customer-education message for Glide is "plan for 8-12 weeks minimum to feel the full effect — daily use, every day." Drop-off at Week 4 is the #1 retention risk; honest expectation-setting at the front of the journey is what protects the LTV math.

Here's what he/she actually experiences across a 3-6 month arc on 2 caps/day (1 morning + 1 evening with meals):

Weeks 1-2: the adjustment window.

Minimal perceived change. The body is calibrating to the curcumin + Boswellia + piperine load. Maybe one or two mornings of marginally easier knee mobility. Often, nothing he/she would consciously register. Some users notice mild GI activity in the first week (curcumin can be activating; BioPerine increases gastric activity); typically resolves by Week 2 when taken with meals.

Weeks 3-6: the first signal.

A meaningful proportion of consistent users start to notice:

  • Morning knee/lower-back stiffness duration reducing (from ~10-15 minutes to ~5-7 minutes)
  • Post-1-hour-sitting stiffness less pronounced when he/she stands
  • Post-yoga / post-exercise soreness recovering faster
  • Finger-joint stiffness easing (women particularly)

The user pattern at this stage: he/she finishes Pack 1, recognises he/she has been moving slightly easier, and decides "let me see if the pattern holds." This is the trial-to-loyal conversion moment.

Weeks 6-12: the full effect lands.

Joint-comfort improvement at the 30-50% subjective range (validated against benchmarks: Daily 2016 meta-analysis on curcumin in OA showed effects comparable to NSAIDs over 8-12 weeks; Kimmatkar 2003 RCT on Boswellia at 333mg 3x/day showed knee pain reduction over 8 weeks). Range-of-motion improving. Sleep quality marginally better (less night-time joint discomfort). The "I feel my knees more than I used to" subjective complaint reduces.

3-6+ months: maintenance and systemic anti-inflammatory compounding.

Sustained joint comfort. Systemic inflammatory markers (CRP if measured) trend down. He/she has settled into Glide as a daily ritual; subscription is on autopilot. The post-30-yoga-class soreness is now manageable, the morning knee stiffness is a distant memory, the orthopaedist visit is a routine annual check rather than a problem-driven consult.

6. Theoretical justification (why each active works)

For each of the 5 actives: mechanism, classical basis, modern clinical evidence with verifiable URLs, dose rationale.

1. Sami-Sabinsa Curcumin C3 Complex (95-97% curcuminoids, 250mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: curcuminoids inhibit COX-2 (cyclooxygenase-2) and modulate NF-kB transcription factor — two of the central inflammation-signalling pathways. Antioxidant via free-radical scavenging. Modulates inflammatory cytokine expression (TNF-alpha, IL-1, IL-6).
  • Classical basis: Turmeric (Haridra, Curcuma longa) is one of the most-cited herbs in Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita. Classical indications include Sandhivata (joint disease), Vatarakta (gouty arthritis), Shotha (inflammation/swelling). Cross-pillar Ayurvedic anti-inflammatory.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Daily JW, Yang M, Park S. "Efficacy of Turmeric Extracts and Curcumin for Alleviating the Symptoms of Joint Arthritis: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Randomized Clinical Trials." Journal of Medicinal Food, 2016; 19(8):717-729. PubMed. 8 RCTs pooled; curcumin 1,000mg/day for 8-12 weeks improved joint pain comparable to NSAIDs in osteoarthritis patients. Plus the broader Aggarwal-lab curcumin literature at MD Anderson (>120 published papers): Aggarwal BB, Sundaram C, Malani N, Ichikawa H. "Curcumin: the Indian solid gold." Adv Exp Med Biol, 2007; 595:1-75. PubMed. Plus Hewlings SJ, Kalman DS. "Curcumin: A Review of Its Effects on Human Health." Foods, 2017; 6(10):92. PMC.
  • Dose rationale: 250 mg/cap × 2 caps = 500 mg curcuminoid extract daily. With BioPerine bioavailability enhancement (~2000% AUC uplift per Shoba 1998), bioavailable curcumin equivalent is in the clinical-effective range of standalone 1,000mg+ curcumin formulations. Lower-clinical-effective dose at the bioavailability-enhanced tier; cost-efficient vs CurcuWIN/Meriva bioavailability formats.

2. Sami-Sabinsa Boswellin Super (65% boswellic acids, 350mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: boswellic acids (especially AKBA — acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswellic acid) inhibit 5-LOX (5-lipoxygenase), reducing leukotriene-mediated inflammation. Distinct mechanism from curcumin's COX-2/NF-kB pathway — the two together address both major inflammatory cascades.
  • Classical basis: Boswellia (Shallaki, Boswellia serrata) is classically indicated in Charaka Samhita for Sandhivata (joint disease) and Amavata (rheumatoid-equivalent). Resin from the Boswellia tree has been used in Ayurvedic anti-inflammatory preparations for millennia.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Kimmatkar N, Thawani V, Hingorani L, Khiyani R. "Efficacy and tolerability of Boswellia serrata extract in treatment of osteoarthritis of knee: a randomized double blind placebo controlled trial." Phytomedicine, 2003; 10(1):3-7. PubMed. 333mg Boswellia 3x/day for 8 weeks reduced knee OA pain significantly vs placebo. Plus Sengupta K et al. "A double blind, randomized, placebo controlled study of the efficacy and safety of 5-Loxin for treatment of osteoarthritis of the knee." Arthritis Res Ther, 2008; 10:R85. PMC. Plus Vishal AA et al. Plus Kizhakkedath R. "Clinical evaluation of a formulation containing Curcuma longa and Boswellia serrata extracts in the management of knee osteoarthritis." Mol Med Rep, 2013; 8(5):1542-1548. PubMed — confirms curcumin + Boswellia combination superior to either alone.
  • Dose rationale: 350 mg/cap × 2 caps = 700 mg Boswellin Super daily, delivering ~455 mg total boswellic acids (vs Kimmatkar 2003 333mg-3x/day = 1,000mg/day at unspecified standardisation). At the premium 65% standardisation, our 700mg/day extract dose puts Glide at the upper end of the clinical-effective range.

3. Turmeric whole-rhizome powder (TGL captive, Erode/Salem cohort, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: whole-rhizome carries non-curcuminoid actives — ar-turmerone, atlantone, zingiberene, alpha-turmerone — that exhibit independent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activity. Classical Ayurvedic full-spectrum delivery.
  • Classical basis: same Charaka / Sushruta foundation as Sami C3 Complex; whole-rhizome is the traditional kashayam / churna delivery form (extracts are a modern format).
  • Modern clinical evidence: Aggarwal BB et al. "Curcumin: An Indian Solid Gold." Adv Exp Med Biol, 2007; 595:1-75. PubMed — covers non-curcuminoid turmeric actives. Plus Park SY, Kim DSHL. "Discovery of natural products from Curcuma longa that protect cells from beta-amyloid insult: a drug discovery effort against Alzheimer's disease." J Nat Prod, 2002; 65(9):1227-1231. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 100 mg/cap × 2 caps = 200 mg/day whole-rhizome. Tier C heritage thread anchoring the existing TGL Turmeric migration cohort; carries traditional Ayurvedic full-spectrum signal alongside the clinical-dose extract.
  • Brand role: the migration story for existing TGL Turmeric buyers requires a visible turmeric heritage thread. Whole-rhizome at 100mg/cap is the "same TGL turmeric you know" anchor that bridges single-actor-turmeric to multi-active joint-care.

4. Sami-Sabinsa BioPerine (95% piperine, 5mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: piperine inhibits hepatic and intestinal glucuronidation of curcumin, dramatically increasing serum curcumin AUC. Without piperine, oral curcumin has low bioavailability (large first-pass metabolism); with piperine, bioavailable curcumin increases ~2000%.
  • Classical basis: black pepper (Maricha, Piper nigrum) is the most-cited anupana (vehicle) in classical Ayurveda for enhancing the action of other herbs. The Ayurvedic concept of bhaisajya (synergy) anchors the piperine + curcumin combination two millennia before the Shoba 1998 modern study.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Shoba G, Joy D, Joseph T, Majeed M, Rajendran R, Srinivas PSSR. "Influence of piperine on the pharmacokinetics of curcumin in animals and human volunteers." Planta Medica, 1998; 64(4):353-356. PubMed. The classic bioavailability study; piperine at 20mg/day increased curcumin AUC 2000% in healthy human volunteers. Industry-standard reference for curcumin formulation since.
  • Dose rationale: 5 mg/cap × 2 caps = 10 mg/day piperine extract (~9.5mg actual piperine at 95% standardisation). Below Shoba 1998 study dose of 20mg/day piperine; well below 20mg/day GI-tolerance ceiling. Effective at this dose for curcumin bioavailability enhancement; conservative for tolerance.

5. Moringa whole-leaf powder (TGL captive, Tier C minimal heritage signature, 12.5mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: whole-plant nutritive (iron, calcium, vitamin C, antioxidants, chlorophyll) — at this Tier C minimal dose, primarily a brand-thesis carrier rather than a primary anti-inflammatory.
  • Folk + modern basis: established nutraceutical literature on moringa as iron + folate + B-complex source.
  • Modern evidence: Alegbeleye OO. "How Functional Is Moringa oleifera? A Review of Its Nutritive, Medicinal, and Socioeconomic Potential." Food Nutr Bull, 2018. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 12.5 mg/cap × 2 caps = 25 mg/day. Tier C minimal heritage signature dose, locked per Moringa Foundation principle (2026-05-05). Substantive enough to carry the moringa thread without overstating in a Tier C extension where Turmeric is the hero.
  • Brand role: heritage signature carrying the cross-portfolio "Built on Moringa. Powered by Ayurveda." narrative.

7. How we arrived at this product (the decision narrative)

Three layers of decisions that produced Glide as the SKU it is.

Layer 1: Why Glide as Tier C / Phase 3 (not Tier A, not later)

We evaluated all 10 SKUs across volume, profitability, TGL-fit, and founder authenticity. Glide scored well on:

  • Migration cohort size: ~10-15K existing TGL Turmeric Amazon buyers in repeat cohort — the largest single-SKU migration target in the catalog after Spark (existing Ashwagandha cohort).
  • Unisex extension: only Tier C SKU that opens the men's-extension door under Pillar 3 without forcing primary men's-health positioning.
  • Premium price-anchor support: at the V3 ₹1,499 MRP, Glide co-anchors the upper price ceiling alongside Crest ₹1,799 — making Cycle ₹999 read accessible-affordable.

Glide scored lower on Phase 1 launchability:

  • Cold-cohort education cost: TGL has no current 35-65 unisex joint-care customer base at the women's-hormonal-continuum lead. Education investment is real.
  • Tier A first: the Q1 trio (Cycle + Bloom + Reset) earns the website-led acquisition motion + brand + price ladder before Tier C extensions ship.

So Glide is sequenced to Phase 3 / batch 3 not because it's a weaker SKU but because it's a Tier C extension that pays off after Tier A sets the brand foundation. The migration economics are best-in-portfolio (existing customer base, not cold acquisition), but the migration motion only works after Phase 1 has proven the Shopify-first playbook.

Layer 3: Why this specific dose architecture (and not another)

Every dose was triangulated across three constraints:

  1. Clinical / classical therapeutic range (what RCTs or Charaka actually use)
  2. Capsule fill feasibility (Size #00 = ~700-1000 mg practical fill)
  3. 2-cap-per-day compliance ceiling (anything beyond 2 caps creates retention friction)

That triangulation forced the Size #00 path (Size #0 cannot hold the 250mg C3 + 350mg Boswellin Super matrix at premium standardisation). At 717 mg fill, capsule remains comfortable and we have headroom for vendor-formulator to add ginger (50mg) at trial-fill if needed.

Trade-offs we accepted, openly stated:

  • Lower-clinical-effective curcumin dose at the bioavailability-enhanced tier, not the 1,000mg+/day standalone-curcumin dose. C3+BioPerine equivalence justifies the lower extract dose; cost-efficiency vs CurcuWIN/Meriva is real. Customer-feedback signal on joint-comfort improvement at Week 12 is the validation gate; if sub-clinical, V2 reformulation lifts curcuminoid extract to 350mg/cap (700mg/day extract).
  • GM at sub price (₹1,147) sits at 69.1% — at the floor, not above. Boswellia generic fallback (Vidya Herbs Boswellin or Arjuna at ~₹1,150/kg vs Sami Boswellin Super ~₹2,800/kg) is the margin-protect lever, restoring sub GM to ~72%. Lever is operational, not strategic.
  • Single Sami-Sabinsa supplier concentration on three of five actives (C3 Complex, Boswellin Super, BioPerine). Mitigated by Vidya Herbs / Arjuna Natural backup paths for Boswellia and piperine; C3 Complex is the only active where Sami is the structural primary.
  • Per-active mg numbers look modest on label vs single-actor 1,000mg curcumin SKUs (Glide reads "Curcumin C3 Complex 250mg/cap" while Himalaya Curcumin reads "Curcumin 250mg/tablet"). Mitigated by the multi-active framing — Glide doesn't compete on curcumin-mg count, it competes on multi-pathway anti-inflammatory stack at clinical doses.
  • Migration friction from existing TGL Turmeric ₹399 to Glide ₹1,349 sale (~3.4x price uplift). Mitigated by explicit upgrade messaging ("same TGL turmeric, plus what your joints need"), bundle pricing for the Turmeric→Glide transition cycle, founder Reels walking the upgrade story.

The decision trail in one sentence: Glide is the upgrade SKU for ~10-15K existing TGL Turmeric Amazon customers and the 35-65 unisex joint-care extension, formulated with branded Sami-Sabinsa C3 Complex + Boswellin Super at clinical-evidenced doses + BioPerine bioavailability + TGL captive turmeric heritage thread, priced at V3 premium ₹1,499 MRP to anchor TGL's premium ceiling, with 76% GM at MRP earning the position.
