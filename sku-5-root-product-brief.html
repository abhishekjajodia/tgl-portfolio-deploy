Root is TGL's daily Ayurvedic hair-care SKU, designed for the 25-50 year old Indian woman dealing with the modern triple stack of hair fall — postpartum telogen effluvium, post-Covid shedding, and stress / PCOS-linked thinning — who has cycled through synthetic-biotin gummies and is looking for a comprehensive Ayurvedic alternative that addresses the root cause, not just the cofactor. 3. The customer (who she is, what she's tried, what fails her)

Who she is (core 28-38, edge 25-50):

She's an urban Indian woman, Tier 1-2 (Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi, Pune, Hyderabad, Chennai expanding nationally), household income ₹6-25L, English-Hindi or English-regional bilingual, on Instagram, follows hair-care creators (Anaysa, Aishwarya Kandpal, Be Bodywise UGC). She is wellness-conscious but supplement-skeptical, reads Earthful and Cosmix content, has one or more friends on Minoxidil topical 2%, has tried at least one biotin gummy, and is now asking the deeper question: "what's actually causing this and how do I fix it from the inside?"

She's at one of three trigger points:

  • Postpartum (0-18 months postpartum): peak telogen effluvium 3-4 months post-delivery; she's distressed and Googling.
  • Post-Covid (still trailing through 2024-26 cohort per Mieczkowska JAAD 2021 — 22% of post-acute-Covid patients reported hair fall): her acute illness has resolved, but the hair fall hasn't.
  • PCOS / stress / hormonal (chronic): 25-40% of PCOS women have androgenic alopecia presentation; chronic stress + sustained cortisol elevation pushes follicles into telogen prematurely.

There's also a secondary persona, 38-50, perimenopausal hair thinning + slower scalp regeneration + dryness/breakage. More clinical, more research-driven, will scrutinise ingredient lists and source provenance. This cohort cross-sells from Bloom (Phase 1 Q1) and Crest (Phase 4 Q4) into Root.

What's happening to her body (the medical context):

Hair fall is rarely one cause. It's almost always a stack of:

  • Iron-deficiency anaemia (~52% of Indian women of reproductive age per NFHS-5, 2019-21) — hair fall is a downstream symptom in deficient women.
  • Cortisol-driven telogen effluvium — sustained stress pushes follicles out of anagen prematurely; effect lags onset by ~3 months.
  • Androgenic-pattern thinning in PCOS — DHT-driven follicle miniaturisation at the crown / parting.
  • Postpartum telogen effluvium — natural estrogen-withdrawal cascade after delivery; resolves in 6-12 months but causes high-distress in 20-40% of new mothers.
  • Nutritional thinness — modern Indian diets are protein-thin, iron-thin, B-vitamin-thin; the hair shaft is the first to show it.

She is not pathologically diagnosed. She has not been to a trichologist. Her dermatologist (if she's seen one) handed her Minoxidil + told her "give it 6 months." Most general practitioners just say "take iron + biotin."

4. What the product does (the lived experience over 3-6 months)

Root is not a 30-day fix, and we don't market it as one. Hair grows ~1cm/month; the follicle cycle is 3+ months minimum. Any hair-care brand promising "results in 30 days" is selling biology that doesn't exist. Setting that expectation honestly is what protects us from the "I took it for two weeks and felt nothing" review wall that kills D2C wellness brands.

Here's what she actually experiences if she takes 2 caps/day for 6 months:

Month 1 (first 30 days): the compliance test.

Minimal perceived change. Maybe a subtle reduction in shedding by week 3-4 (BacoMind Bacopa adaptogen action accumulating, PrimaVie Shilajit micronutrient repletion starting). Often, nothing she'd register. This is the highest drop-off window in any hair-care D2C; most customers quit here. The marketing job in Month 1 is not to sell efficacy; it's to sell patience. Deepika's content arc in launch month is "hair grows 1cm a month — that's biology, not marketing. Give it 3 months before you decide."

Months 2-3 (60-90 days): the first signal.

A meaningful proportion of consistent users start to report:

  • Visible reduction in shower-drain hair / comb-pull hair count
  • Fewer strands on the pillow in the morning
  • "Baby hairs" appearing at the hairline (new anagen-phase follicles cycling through)
  • Scalp feels less dry / less inflamed (Manjistha + curry leaf microcirculation effect)

The user pattern at this stage: she finishes Pack 2, recognises she felt better last month than the one before, and decides "let me see if it sticks." This is the conversion moment from trial to loyal customer. Subscription retention math says: if she finishes Pack 2 and reorders Pack 3, she's typically a 6-9 month customer.

Months 3-6 (90-180 days): density recovery.

For users with the dominant trigger profile (postpartum / post-stress / mild PCOS), Root starts to show measurable density recovery. Parting line less visible, ponytail circumference returning, hair feels thicker at root. Cyclical shedding events (luteal-phase shedding for cycling women) less acute. The "I forgot how much hair I used to have" moment lands somewhere in this window.

6+ months: maintenance and step-down.

Once density is recovered, some users step down from full daily dosing to alternate-day dosing. Pack consumption stretches from 1 month to 1.5-2 months. Subscription cadence adjusts; annual revenue per customer drops modestly, but lifetime stays high. Others stay on full daily dose for the consistency. We don't push customers either way; the product trains its own dose.

6. Theoretical justification (why each active works)

For each of the 6 actives: mechanism, classical basis, modern clinical evidence with verifiable URLs, dose rationale.

1. Bhringraj extract (8:1 from whole-leaf, 2.5% wedelolactone, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: wedelolactone + flavonoids + tannins. Promotes anagen-phase hair follicle cycling; documented angiogenic + hair-follicle-induction activity in animal models. Classical Ayurvedic kesha-raja (king of hair).
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita, Bhavaprakasha. Primary classical hair tonic across the Ayurvedic materia medica. Combined with Brahmi + Manjistha in classical hair formulations (Ashtanga Hridayam, Bhaishajya Ratnavali).
  • Modern clinical evidence: Datta K et al. "Eclipta alba extract with potential for hair growth promoting activity." J Ethnopharmacol, 2009 / Arch Dermatol Res. PubMed. Ethanolic Eclipta alba extract induced more anagen follicles than 2% Minoxidil in a C57BL/6 mouse model.
  • Dose rationale: 100mg/cap × 2 caps = 200mg daily extract = ~1,600mg whole-plant equivalent (Datta 2009 mouse-model dose extrapolated to ~250-300mg human-equivalent; 200mg is the conservative entry dose, vendor-formulator can lift to 250-300mg at trial-fill if economics support).

2. BacoMind Bacopa (Natural Remedies, 50% bacosides, 50mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: bacosides A + B (active triterpenoid saponin glycosides). Adaptogen; modulates HPA axis cortisol response. Cortisol-driven telogen effluvium is a documented hair-fall driver — sustained stress pushes follicles into telogen phase prematurely (effect lags onset by ~3 months, recovers after stressor resolves + Bacopa 60-90 day window).
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita primary Medhya Rasayana (cognitive tonic). Bhavaprakasha.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Calabrese C et al. "Effects of a standardized Bacopa monnieri extract on cognitive performance, anxiety, and depression in the elderly." J Altern Complement Med, 2008; 14(6):707-713. PubMed. Plus the Stough 2001 (Psychopharmacology) + Roodenrys 2002 cognitive-RCT lineage that BacoMind specifically has been used in.
  • Dose rationale: 50mg/cap × 2 caps = 100mg daily extract = 50mg bacosides/day. Sub-clinical for cognition primary (which requires 300-600mg bacosides/day per the cognitive-RCT lineage), supportive for stress / scalp circulation context. The branded BacoMind attribution (Natural Remedies, NOT Sabinsa Bacopin) is the citable clinical-trial leverage.

3. PrimaVie Shilajit (Natreon, ≥50% fulvic acid + 10.3% DBPs, 50mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: dibenzo-α-pyrones (DBPs) + fulvic acid + 80+ trace minerals. Mitochondrial-CoQ10-stabilising effect (Stohs 2017). Documented bioavailability enhancement of co-administered actives. Anti-inflammatory + adaptogenic.
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita — Shilajatu as primary Rasayana. Long-standing classical adjunct in hair-fall formulations.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Pandit S et al. "Clinical evaluation of purified Shilajit on testosterone levels in healthy volunteers." Andrologia, 2016; 48(5):570-575. PubMed. Plus Stohs SJ et al. "Safety and efficacy of Shilajit (mumie, moomiyo)." Phytother Res, 2014. PubMed — review of PrimaVie clinical evidence base.
  • Dose rationale: 50mg/cap × 2 caps = 100mg daily — sub-clinical adjunctive (Pandit 2016 used 250mg/day for testosterone clinical effect; full clinical dose lives in Spark). At 100mg/day, PrimaVie's role in Root is micronutrient + adaptogenic + bioavailability-enhancement of co-administered Bhringraj + Bacopa.
  • Why NABL heavy-metal clearance is non-negotiable: raw Shilajit collected from Himalayan sources can carry mercury, lead, arsenic at unsafe levels. PrimaVie's purified clinical-grade processing eliminates this risk (NABL-accredited per-batch testing). This is why PrimaVie is the only Shilajit ingredient TGL will use, despite the cost premium vs commodity Shilajit.

4. Manjistha extract (8:1 from whole-root, ~10% anthraquinones, 25mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: anthraquinones + glycosides. Classical Raktashodhaka (blood purifier). Modern: anti-inflammatory + lymphatic decongestant + scalp microcirculation support. Paired with Bhringraj in classical hair formulations.
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita primary Raktaprasadana herb. Bhavaprakasha + Sushruta both feature it.
  • Modern clinical evidence: anti-inflammatory in vitro studies; hair-fall-specific clinical data limited. Traditional-strong, modern-emerging. No specific landmark clinical paper at this stage.
  • Dose rationale: 25mg/cap × 2 caps = 50mg daily = ~200mg whole-root equivalent (modern Ayurvedic supplement dose typically 100-300mg whole-root).

5. Curry leaf extract (carbazole alkaloid standardisation, 50mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: carbazole alkaloids (mahanimbine, koenimbine) + flavonoids + iron + B-complex (in whole leaf). Classical South Indian hair-care narrative ("curry leaf for thick black hair"). Modern: antioxidant + supportive phytochemical contribution.
  • Classical basis: Bhavaprakasha. Folk + classical hair-care application across South Indian Ayurvedic tradition.
  • Modern clinical evidence: hair-fall-specific clinical data thin. Brand-story + supportive-phytochemical role in the formulation, not primary clinical lever.
  • Dose rationale: 50mg/cap × 2 caps = 100mg daily extract.
  • Important caveat: NOT iron-standardised. No Indian vendor offers iron-standardised Murraya koenigii at clinical-grade. The iron narrative for hair-fall is supportive (delivered via moringa whole-leaf at ~1-2 mg natural Fe/day), NOT therapeutic. Iron-deficient hair-fall users should pair Root with Bloom (daily multi) or with practitioner-prescribed iron supplementation. This is an honesty point we make on the PDP.

6. Moringa whole-leaf powder (heat-treated, TGL captive supply, 50mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: whole-plant nutritive (iron ~1-2 mg/day at this dose, calcium, vitamin C, antioxidants, chlorophyll). Not a primary hair-fall active; supports nutritional baseline + carries the brand heritage thread.
  • Folk + modern basis: established nutraceutical literature on moringa as iron + folate + B-complex source. Iron deficiency exacerbates hair fall; many menstruating Indian women are sub-clinically deficient.
  • Modern evidence: Alegbeleye OO. "How Functional Is Moringa oleifera? A Review of Its Nutritive, Medicinal, and Socioeconomic Potential." Food Nutr Bull, 2018. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 50mg/cap × 2 caps = 100mg daily. Tier B 50mg signature dose per Moringa Foundation principle. Substantive enough to carry the moringa thread; calibrated against Deepika's stacking-overload concern.
  • Brand role: heritage signature carrying the cross-portfolio narrative.

7. How we arrived at this product (the decision narrative)

Three layers of decisions that produced Root as the SKU it is.

Layer 1: Why Root as Phase 2 Q2 (not Phase 1, not later)

We evaluated all 10 SKUs across volume, profitability, TGL-fit, and founder authenticity. Root scored high on cross-sell anchor potential and ad-funnel CAC efficiency, but lower than Cycle on Phase 1 launchability:

  • 90-day-to-effect category, not 1-2 cycles. Cycle proves fast (1-2 cycles); Root needs 90 days minimum. Phase 1 needs anchor SKUs that prove fast.
  • Bundling with Phase 1 Q1 SKUs is the activation lever. Bloom + Root (Daily Woman Kit) and Cycle + Root (Cycle Glow Kit) are the two highest-attach bundle picks. Root needs Phase 1 SKUs in market to bundle against.
  • Hair fall is the #2 customer-voice in TGL's existing review base. Already-warm cohort waiting for the formulation; sequencing to Q2 doesn't lose the cohort, it just delays the conversion.

So Root is sequenced to Phase 2 Q2 not because it's a weaker SKU but because the bundle-attach economics depend on Phase 1 SKUs being live first.

Layer 3: Why this specific dose architecture (and not another)

Every dose was triangulated across three constraints:

  1. Clinical / classical therapeutic range (what RCTs or Bhavaprakasha actually use)
  2. Capsule fill feasibility (Size #0 = ~500mg max practical fill)
  3. 2-cap-per-day compliance ceiling (anything beyond 2 caps creates retention friction)

That triangulation forced the extract-first decision (Operational Principle 6, locked as TGL-P1-DEC-011). With 5 extracts + 1 whole-leaf base at 8:1 concentration on the Bhringraj + Manjistha extracts, we deliver classical-equivalent doses (Bhringraj ~1,600 mg whole-plant equivalent, Manjistha ~200mg whole-root equivalent) while staying inside the 325mg fill × 2 caps/day envelope.

Trade-offs we accepted, openly stated:

  • BacoMind + PrimaVie are sub-clinical doses in this SKU. BacoMind 100mg/day is below the cognitive-RCT range (300-600mg bacosides/day); PrimaVie 100mg/day is below the testosterone-RCT dose (250mg/day). Full clinical doses for both live in Spark (Phase 6 Q3 ashwagandha+ stress SKU). In Root, both are adjunctive supportive doses with branded-citable evidence — the BacoMind / PrimaVie attribution gives us the clinical-trial leverage even at sub-clinical dose, which is how the ingredient-literate hair-care customer reads ingredient lists.
  • Curry leaf is NOT iron-standardised. No Indian vendor offers it at clinical-grade. The iron narrative is supportive (moringa whole-leaf), not primary. Honesty point on the PDP — iron-deficient hair-fall users should pair with Bloom or practitioner-prescribed iron.
  • Sesbania-biotin deferred from V1. Supplier landscape unclear at brief-lock; can revisit at trial-fill. The biotin-narrative gap is intentional — we're not competing with synthetic-biotin gummy brands, we're competing with the comprehensive Ayurvedic stack at premium tier.
  • Per-active mg numbers look small on label (Manjistha 25mg/cap reads weak). Mitigated by labelling whole-plant equivalence on-pack ("Manjistha extract 25mg, equivalent to 200mg whole-root").
  • Single Indian distributor (specialty channel) for PrimaVie + BacoMind concentration risk. Mitigated by both Natural Remedies (BacoMind) and Natreon (PrimaVie India distributor) being established specialty-botanical relationships with multi-year India-supply track records. Sabinsa Bacopin + Sabinsa Shilajit Extract are retained as cost-fallback paths if specialty channels stall (with explicit brand-narrative compromise — we'd need to step back the clinical-evidence claim).

The decision trail in one sentence: Root is the cross-sell anchor that converts TGL's already-buying-moringa-for-hair customer base into a comprehensive Ayurvedic hair-care commitment; the 6-active stack pairs the classical kesha-raja Bhringraj + Manjistha duo with two clinical-grade branded actives (BacoMind Bacopa from Natural Remedies + PrimaVie Shilajit from Natreon) at the premium ₹1,199 MRP tier where no peer currently exists; the +₹100 lift from V2 reflects the genuine COGS step-up from the dual-branded actives, earning the premium positioning.
