What's happening to her body (the medical context):

Postpartum is the most demanding nutrient-export job her body will ever do. The clinical realities:

  • Iron deficiency: postpartum is the highest-risk window for anemia in women's lifespan due to delivery blood loss + ongoing lactation iron drain. ICMR estimates 60%+ of postpartum Indian women are iron-deficient. Sub-clinical anemia exacerbates fatigue, hair fall, and (per emerging research) milk-supply concerns.
  • Calcium drain: ~200mg Ca/day is excreted in breastmilk; calcium reserves drop ~3-5% during lactation if dietary intake is inadequate. The mother's bone-mineral density takes a hit that takes 6-12 months to recover post-weaning.
  • Lactational amenorrhea: 6-12 month average; estrogen-suppressed state has unique nutrient demands different from her cycling years.
  • Postpartum thyroiditis: ~5-10% of postpartum women experience subclinical hypothyroidism in the 0-12 month window, with hair fall, fatigue, and low milk supply as downstream effects.
  • Postpartum depression / mood: ~10-15% of postpartum Indian women report PPD symptoms; the adaptogenic herbs in classical Stree-rasayana formulations have long been used as supportive (not treatment).

The use case is real and clinical, not marketing-fabricated. Nourish addresses a genuine nutritional and physiological deficit, not a manufactured one.

4. What the product does (the lived experience over 3-6 months)

Nourish is supportive nutrition during the most demanding nutrient-export job her body will ever do. It is not a milk-supply rescue for clinical lactation insufficiency (those mothers need lactation consultants + medical evaluation, not a supplement). It is not a postpartum-depression treatment (that needs clinical care). It is not a substitute for diet, sleep, hydration, or breastfeeding-on-demand. What it is, honestly: a comprehensive nutritional and Ayurvedic galactagogue support stack that compounds over weeks and months in ways that matter to a mother who is already doing everything else right.

Here's what she actually experiences if she takes 3 caps/day with meals for 6 months:

Days 1-7 (the nutritional repletion window):

For mothers with baseline iron deficiency (the dominant subset of postpartum Indian women), Days 1-7 may bring early relief from iron-deficiency fatigue: slightly more energy, less of the "I can't keep my eyes open at 3pm" exhaustion. This is not Nourish-specific; it's iron-baseline support kicking in. The galactagogue layer (Shatavari + Methi + moringa) hasn't yet had time to compound. Marketing should not over-promise Day 1-7 milk-supply changes.

Shatavari at 500mg/day total + Methi 200mg/day + moringa 400mg/day galactagogue dose start to compound. Many mothers report "fuller-feeling breasts" between feeds, "baby seems satisfied longer between feeds," or simply "I feel like supply is more settled." This is the period where the galactagogue effect crosses the perceptibility threshold for mothers who had genuine perceived undersupply. For mothers whose supply was already adequate, Nourish maintains supply during what would otherwise be a natural mid-lactation dip.

Weeks 4-8 (cumulative postpartum recovery):

Iron + calcium + galactagogue + supportive phytochemical layer compounds. Postpartum hair shedding stabilises (it doesn't reverse — that takes longer — but the worst of the hair-fall window starts to settle). Bone-mineral support from Aquamin-Ca starts the slow process of replenishment. Mood + energy stabilise as cumulative nutritional repletion catches up to lactation drain. 5. Composition + formulation logic

Five actives in the cap, organised as one moringa whole-leaf heritage base (at the Tier B galactagogue exception dose) + four standardised extracts and natural-source minerals. This is TGL's extract-first formulation philosophy in action (per 02-brand-dna.md Operational Principle 6, locked 2026-05-09 as TGL-P1-DEC-011), with the documented Tier B exception for moringa per the Moringa Foundation principle.

Format: Size #0 capsule × 3 caps/day daily (split morning + lunch + evening with meals per DEC-014). The 3-caps-per-day path is locked because: (a) postpartum users tolerate splitting doses across meals well (they're already taking everything with food to manage GI and iron-absorption), (b) split-meal galactagogue dosing improves blood-level consistency for the Shatavari + Methi saponins, and (c) preserving therapeutic doses takes priority over capsule-size minimisation per DEC-014. 6. Theoretical justification (why each active works)

For each of the 5 actives: mechanism, classical basis, modern clinical evidence with verifiable URLs, dose rationale.

1. Shatavari extract (4-5% saponins, 167mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: phytoestrogenic steroidal saponins (shatavarins I-IV) modulate prolactin secretion via dopamine antagonism at the pituitary, supporting milk synthesis. Adaptogenic action on the hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian (HPO) axis aids postpartum hormonal recalibration.
  • Classical basis: Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, Bhavaprakasha Nighantu. Primary Stree-rasayana (women's tonic) and the most-cited Ayurvedic galactagogue (Stanya-vardhaka). Foundational ingredient in classical lactation formulations.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Sharma S. et al. "Randomized controlled trial of Asparagus racemosus (Shatavari) as a lactogogue in lactational inadequacy." Indian Pediatrics, 1996. Plus Patel L., Kanole P. "Asparagus racemosus (Shatavari) in lactational inadequacy: a clinical study." Indian J Pharmacol, 2014. Both showed shatavari-standardized extracts improved both milk supply AND prolactin levels in lactating mothers. Meta-context: Pandey A.K. et al. "Impact of stress on female reproductive health disorders: Possible beneficial effects of shatavari (Asparagus racemosus)." Biomed Pharmacother, 2018; 103:46-49. PubMed.
  • Dose rationale: 167mg/cap × 3 caps = 500mg/day extract. Delivers ~20-25mg shatavarins/day, in the high-end therapeutic galactagogue range (clinical lactation trials use 200-500mg standardised extract daily).

2. Methi (fenugreek) extract (4-5% saponins, 67mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: trigonelline + diosgenin precursors + steroidal saponins. Galactagogue mechanism not fully elucidated but hypothesised as phytoestrogenic + direct mammary-tissue modulation. Conservative dose well below the threshold (500-1000mg+) at which neonatal maple-syrup-urine-like odor or GI symptoms have been reported.
  • Classical basis: Cross-cultural galactagogue. In Ayurveda, methi (Methika) is documented in Bhavaprakasha as a postpartum tonic (methi laddu is the everyday-kitchen Indian postpartum food). In Mediterranean and Middle Eastern traditions, fenugreek tea + seed preparations are traditional postpartum.
  • Modern clinical evidence: Turkyilmaz C. et al. "The effect of galactagogue herbal tea on breast milk production and short-term catch-up of birth weight in the first week of life." J Altern Complement Med, 2011; 17(2):139-142. PubMed. Showed fenugreek tea (~600mg/day equivalent) increased milk volume in healthy lactating women in the first week postpartum. Multiple corroborating studies in global breastfeeding-medicine literature.
  • Dose rationale: 67mg/cap × 3 caps = 200mg/day extract. Delivers ~8-10mg saponins/day, supportive galactagogue layer paired with the primary Shatavari dose. Conservative dose maintains regulatory + safety clarity (well below the 500mg+ threshold where neonatal effects become a concern). Trade-off accepted: we lose some galactagogue efficacy at this conservative dose but gain a defensible safety profile and clean Schedule VI compliance for the lactation context.

3. Aquamin-Ca (algae lithothamnion calcium, 100mg/cap)

  • Mechanism: natural-source plant-derived calcium from red algae (Lithothamnion calcareum, Marigot-sourced). Multi-mineral matrix delivers bioavailable calcium plus 70+ trace minerals. Calcium contributes to maintenance of normal bones, teeth, muscle function, and neurotransmission.
  • Classical basis: Aquamin-Ca is a modern natural-source mineral product, not a classical Ayurvedic active. Used in Nourish + Bloom + Crest as TGL's natural-source calcium delivery vehicle.
  • Modern evidence: Felice V.D. et al. "A natural multi-mineral formula reduces inflammation in chondrocytes." Magnes Res, 2018. Plus established calcium-and-bone literature for lactation-specific calcium support. FSSAI-recognised structure-function claim for calcium: "contributes to maintenance of normal bones."
  • Dose rationale: 100mg/cap × 3 caps = 300mg extract = ~100mg elemental Ca/day. Supportive 10% RDA for lactating women (1,000mg/day RDA), complementary to dietary intake. Not a full-RDA replacement; framed honestly as supportive postpartum calcium.

4. Moringa whole-leaf powder (heat-treated, TGL captive supply, 133mg/cap — Tier B galactagogue exception)

  • Mechanism: whole-plant nutritive (iron, calcium, vitamin A, B-complex, vitamin C, antioxidants, chlorophyll) PLUS clinically-documented galactagogue effect at 400mg+/day. Multi-pathway support: nutritional repletion + direct galactagogue action.
  • Classical basis: established Ayurvedic + folk-traditional galactagogue. Moringa leaf preparations (Murungai keerai in Tamil tradition, Sahjan in Hindi tradition) are everyday postpartum kitchen foods across Indian regions.
  • Modern evidence: Estrella M.C.P. et al. "A double-blind, randomised controlled trial on the use of malunggay (Moringa oleifera) for augmentation of the volume of breastmilk among non-nursing mothers of preterm infants." Layer 3: Why this specific dose architecture (and not another)

Every dose was triangulated across four constraints:

  1. Classical therapeutic galactagogue range (Shatavari 200-500mg/day clinical; Methi 100-1000mg/day with safety inflection at 500mg+; moringa 400mg/day per Estrella 2000)
  2. Postpartum safety margin (Methi conservative; Ashwagandha excluded; iron natural-source-only)
  3. Capsule fill feasibility + UX (Size #0 × 3 caps/day per DEC-014 vs Size #00 × 2 with granulation)
  4. COGS + margin (V3 verified ~₹168/pack delivers 86% GM at MRP ₹1,199 — excellent profile)

That triangulation forced the 3-cap Size #0 path locked per DEC-014. With 4 standardised extracts/minerals + 1 whole-leaf moringa base at galactagogue dose, we deliver classical-equivalent + clinical-equivalent doses while staying inside the 500mg fill × 3 caps/day envelope and respecting the lactation-context safety profile.

Trade-offs we accepted, openly stated:

  • 3 caps/day is more than the 2 caps/day high-priority UX target. Mitigated by split-meal dosing (1 morning + 1 lunch + 1 evening with food), which postpartum mothers tolerate well and which improves galactagogue absorption. Per DEC-014, therapeutic-dose preservation > capsule-count minimisation.
  • Methi conservative dose may under-deliver galactagogue efficacy for some mothers. Mitigated by the Shatavari + moringa galactagogue layers carrying the primary load. Open question for Dr. Lakshmi Kala on whether to revisit Methi dose at 6-month customer-feedback review.
  • Iron is supportive (~3-5mg/day natural Fe), not full RDA (18mg/day). Mitigated by honest customer message: "natural-source iron support, complement with prescribed iron if your doctor recommends." Anaemic mothers get directed to clinical iron supplementation, not Nourish-as-iron-replacement.
  • Calcium is supportive (~100mg/day, 10% RDA). Mitigated by similar honest framing; complementary to dietary intake, not replacement.
  • Lodhra-style or Vitex-style hormonal modulation excluded because postpartum hormonal context is different from cycling-women context; HPO-axis modulation is handled by Shatavari at the postpartum-appropriate level, not by adding cycling-women hormonal modulators.

The decision trail in one sentence: Nourish is the SKU TGL's existing mothers were already buying moringa for, designed as the comprehensive Ayurvedic + natural-source-mineral postpartum + lactation capsule that respects both the therapeutic-dose biology and the postpartum safety profile, in a 3-cap split-meal format that fits the meal-time supplement routine new mothers already have.

Lactation-specific regulatory considerations (Nourish-specific, requires regulatory counsel review pre-launch):

  1. No formal "lactation supplement" category in Indian FSSAI framework. Nourish falls under general health-supplement / nutraceutical category. Lactation positioning communicated via structure-function claims, not regulatory category badge.

  2. Galactagogue claim language sensitivity:

    • Permitted: "supports lactation" / "helps maintain healthy milk supply" / "nutritional support for breastfeeding mothers" / "traditional postpartum nourishment" / "iron contributes to normal red blood cell formation" / "calcium contributes to maintenance of normal bones" / "helps mothers recover from the demands of lactation"
    • Avoid in label: "increases milk supply" / "galactagogue" / "lactation enhancer" / "treats low milk supply" / "cures lactation insufficiency" / "replaces formula" / "safe for baby"
    • Educational content can use "galactagogue" with substantiation; label cannot.

  3. Pregnancy contraindication MUST be explicit:

    • Several actives are TRADITIONALLY contraindicated in pregnancy (Shatavari technically, Methi high-dose, Ashwagandha if added)
    • Label must state: "Not for use during pregnancy. For use by lactating mothers in the postpartum period."
    • Marketing copy must clearly distinguish pregnancy vs postpartum/lactation.

  4. Allergen disclosure (mandatory):

    • Methi (fenugreek) is a legume; mandatory allergen declaration under FSSAI Packaging & Labelling Regulations
    • Algae-source minerals: disclose for transparency though not formally an FSSAI-mandated allergen

  5. Pediatrician-consultation language (defensive):

    • Label includes: "Consult your healthcare provider, including your pediatrician, before use during lactation. Some ingredients may pass to breastmilk and affect infants in rare cases."

  6. Specific active warnings:

    • Methi rare reports of neonatal maple-syrup-urine-like odor + GI symptoms in infants of mothers consuming high-dose fenugreek (>500mg/day). Our 200mg/day dose is well below threshold; precautionary label note included.
    • Shatavari traditional Ayurveda holds lactation-safe at moderate doses; no specific warning beyond standard "consult provider."

  7. Marketing imagery restrictions:

    • Avoid imagery suggesting medical-grade clearance (no doctors-with-stethoscope, no hospital settings)
    • Authentic mother imagery preferred (real customers, signed consent for usage)
    • Do not depict baby breastfeeding directly with the product — interpreted as endorsing direct infant exposure to the supplement

  8. Customer service training:

    • Customer support team trained NOT to give medical advice
    • Standard response template for milk-supply queries: "Many mothers find Nourish supportive in their postpartum journey. If you have specific concerns about milk supply, please consult a lactation consultant or your doctor."
